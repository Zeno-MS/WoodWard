## Part 1: Formal Right of Reply Questions (To Superintendent / Communications Director)

*These questions are designed for the formal Right of Reply process prior to publication of Article 3. They provide the district an opportunity to explain the anomalies in public data and address the systemic reliance on premium-priced staffing vendors.*

**Introduction:**
As part of our upcoming investigative series on Vancouver Public Schools' special education funding and staffing models (FY20 - FY25), we are providing the district with a formal Right of Reply. Our analysis of state S-275 personnel data, SAO audits, OSPI records, and district accounts payable ledgers has raised several questions. We want to ensure the district's perspective and operational realities are accurately represented.

**Questions:**

**1. The $76.5 Million Vendor Black Box (ESD 112 Loophole):**  
Our analysis of district accounts payable warrants from FY20 through FY25 shows precisely **$76,576,385.00** routed to four primary out-of-state staffing agencies:
*   Amergis Healthcare Staffing: $39,039,941.67
*   Maxim Healthcare Services: $28,215,062.08
*   ProCare Therapy: $6,841,466.80
*   Soliant Health: $2,472,914.44

Our SQLite queries confirm that out of the hundreds of warrants issued to these vendors, exactly **zero** include line-item descriptions identifying the specific roles procured (`SELECT * WHERE memo IS NULL OR memo = ''`). Furthermore, a comprehensive full-text search of 5,367 BoardDocs files (`("Amergis" OR "Maxim" OR "Soliant" OR "ProCare") AND ("contract" OR "MSA")`) reveals no Master Service Agreements or rate sheets ever presented to the Board. 
*   Did the district utilize the ESD 112 interlocal agreement to bypass a direct, local RFP process for these $76.5M in expenditures? 
*   How does the district justify the lack of itemized role-level oversight (e.g., Paraeducator vs. SLP) for this magnitude of public spending?

**2. Vendor Rate Premiums:**  
The data indicates the district paid significant hourly premiums for contracted staff compared to the fully loaded cost of internal W-2 employees. How does the district justify paying these premiums to private equity-backed firms while simultaneously executing reductions in force (RIFs) for internal staff?

**3. The $5,000 Conversion Fee:**  
Our research indicates that some staffing vendor contracts include a "conversion fee" (often around $5,000) if the district wishes to hire a contracted worker as a permanent internal employee. Can the district confirm if it has paid conversion fees to Soliant, Maxim/Amergis, or other agencies during the FY20-FY25 period? Does the district view these contract terms as a structural barrier to building internal capacity?

**4. Compliance Anxieties and Prohibited Restraints:**  
InvestigateWest reporting and the WA Attorney General's 2018-19 resolution agreement highlighted systemic issues with disproportionate discipline and the continued use of prohibited restraints (post-2021 state ban). Our vector queries of BoardDocs (`("Amergis" OR "Soliant") AND ("onboarding" OR "training" OR "de-escalation")`) suggest a disconnect between vendor onboarding and district de-escalation standards. To what extent did the pressure to resolve these severe compliance findings drive the district's increased reliance on external staffing vendors marketed as rapid "compliance solutions"?

**5. SPED Leadership Turnover:**  
The district has experienced significant turnover in Special Services leadership (Bergeron, Bettis, Phelps, Arkoosh/Laurenzo) during this period of escalating vendor spend. Our analysis indicates a correlated spike in Object-7 (Purchased Services) disbursements during these transition periods. How has this instability in leadership impacted the district's ability to implement long-term structural reforms rather than relying on short-term, premium-priced contracted labor?

**6. SB 5263 Funding Cap Repeal:**  
With the legislature repealing the 16% special education funding cap (SB 5263) and injecting $870 million in new funding into the system, what is the district's concrete plan to transition away from dependency on private staffing agencies and reinvest that new funding into permanent, internal capacity?

## Part 2: Public Records Request (PRR) Draft

*These requests use precise legal framing under the Washington State Public Records Act (RCW 42.56) to compel the production of documents the district has kept out of the public purview, specifically targeting the limitations of BoardDocs.*

**To:** Public Records Officer, Vancouver Public Schools
**Subject:** Public Records Request under RCW 42.56 – Special Education Staffing and Vendor Contracts

Pursuant to the Washington State Public Records Act (RCW 42.56), I am requesting electronic copies of the following public records generated, received, or maintained by Vancouver Public Schools between September 1, 2019 (Beginning of FY 2019-20) and the date this request is fulfilled. 

*Note: Our analysis of the district's public BoardDocs portal confirms that none of the requested documents below have been digitally indexed for public search.*

**1. Vendor Contracts and Rate Sheets:**
*   All Master Service Agreements (MSAs), contracts, contract amendments, renewals, statements of work, and explicitly attached **rate sheets/pricing schedules** for the following vendors:
    *   Soliant Health (and associated entities)
    *   Maxim Healthcare Services / Amergis
    *   ProCare Therapy
    *   The Stepping Stones Group
    *   Suna Solutions

**2. Procurement and Bidding Artifacts:**
*   All Request for Proposals (RFPs), bid tabulations, scoring rubrics, quote documentation, and sole source or emergency procurement justifications associated with the contracts requested in Item 1.
*   If the district utilized an interlocal agreement (e.g., via ESD 112) to procure these services without a direct district-level RFP, please provide all memorandums of understanding or signed agreements executing the use of that cooperative purchasing vehicle for these specific vendors.

**3. Detailed Vendor Invoicing:**
*   Any internal ledger, spreadhseet, or detailed invoice record from the vendors listed in Item 1 that includes a **line-item breakdown** demonstrating the specific roles billed (e.g., "Paraeducator", "SLP"), the hourly rate charged, and the hours worked. (Note: Names of individual contracted practitioners may be redacted under applicable privacy exemptions, but the role, rate, and hours must remain visible).

**4. Internal Service Delivery and Compliance Dashboards:**
*   Any aggregated internal reports or dashboards generated by or for the Special Services department tracking missed IEP service minutes or timeline compliance for special education evaluations. (Must be aggregated/de-identified to protect student privacy).

**5. HR and Staffing Metrics:**
*   Blank copies of exit interview instruments provided to departing special education staff.
*   Any aggregated trend reports or summaries of exit interview data for special education staff.
*   Monthly or quarterly HR reports detailing special education vacancy rates and average "time-to-fill" metrics.

**6. OSPI Correspondence:**
*   All correspondence (emails, letters, memos) between Vancouver Public Schools (specifically the Superintendent, Chief Financial Officer, or Executive Director of Special Services) and the Office of Superintendent of Public Instruction (OSPI) regarding apportionment advances, registered warrants, or cash-flow intervention.

If you anticipate that fulfilling this entire request will take longer than 15 business days, please fulfill the request in installments, prioritizing Items 1, 2, and 3.

If you claim any exemptions to redact or withhold any portion of these records, please provide an exemption log citing the specific statutory exemption claimed as required by RCW 42.56.
