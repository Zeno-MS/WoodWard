## SENTINEL'S CHALLENGES (verbatim, with Antigravity data responses below each)

### Challenge 1: COVID-19 Baseline Distortion
> "The 2020-2021 school year was heavily impacted by COVID-19 remote learning, meaning the need for in-building substitute paras, behavioral specialists, and nurses was artificially at rock-bottom."

**ANTIGRAVITY RESPONSE:** Valid. See data response in Dispatch #18.

### Challenge 2: Federally Mandated IDEA Compliance
> "The $11.89 million spent in 24-25 is the cost of federally mandated compliance. If the district cannot hire an SLP or Special Education teacher due to a historic national labor shortage, IDEA laws still require them to provide those services."

**ANTIGRAVITY RESPONSE:** This is the strongest available defense. Cannot be rebutted without the MSA/invoices (PRR #1) showing role-level detail. However, the Internal Cost Model (Dispatch #14) establishes that even if compliance compels the hiring, the *magnitude* of the premium paid is the investigative question — not whether to use agencies at all.

### Challenge 3: Vendor Consolidation as Good Governance
> "Maintaining a consistent relationship with a primary vendor who can reliably fill hard-to-staff special education roles is arguably better governance."

**ANTIGRAVITY RESPONSE:** This argument actually strengthens the Board oversight question. A $30M cumulative single-vendor relationship over 6 years without apparent formal Board procurement approval is the exact opposite of "good governance" under WA State procurement law (RCW 28A.335.190). The counter isn't about whether the vendor is good — it's about whether anyone was watching.

### Challenge 4: Peer District Staffing Agency Share Unknown
> "We do not know Evergreen's staffing agency share of that Object 7 budget."

**ANTIGRAVITY RESPONSE:** Correct. This is a hard gap. We can only compare total Object 7, not the staffing sub-component, without filing peer PRRs. Sentinel is right that if Evergreen shows a similar explosion, this becomes a regional labor story rather than a VPS-specific scandal.

### Challenge 5: SPED Teacher Blind Spot
> "If a significant portion of that $11.89 million is going specifically toward Special Education teachers, it severely weakens the Accountability Void framing."

**ANTIGRAVITY RESPONSE:** Correct. This is exactly why PRR #1 (itemized invoices) is mission-critical. Without role-level disaggregation, we cannot determine whether the money went to SPED teachers (defensible) vs. paraeducators (less defensible premium) vs. administrative/support staff (indefensible).

### Challenge 6: "Phantom Position" Premium Fallacy
> "You cannot claim lost efficiency if the internal baseline is an unfillable phantom position."

**ANTIGRAVITY RESPONSE:** Partially valid. The cost model should be reframed as: "This is what the district *would* save if it invested in competitive recruitment rather than outsourcing." The premium metric remains useful as a measure of *what the agency extracts*, not necessarily what the district could have avoided.

### Challenge 7: Total Budget Contextualization
> "What percentage of the overall $324 million budget does this $11.89 million agency spend actually represent?"

**ANTIGRAVITY RESPONSE:** See data response in Dispatch #18.

### Challenge 8: HR Recruitment Effort Unknown
> "Did the district's internal HR department run continuous, aggressive recruitment campaigns?"

**ANTIGRAVITY RESPONSE:** This is a PRR/interview question. Cannot be answered from public data alone.
