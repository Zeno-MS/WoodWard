## 5. RCW 28A.335.190 — STATUTORY ANALYSIS

**Source:** `RCW 28A.335.190.pdf` (Certified 7/12/2024), extracted from project root.

### Key Provisions

- **§(1):** Competitive bidding required for "furniture, supplies, equipment, building, improvements, or repairs" at or above threshold levels
- **§(2):** Purchases estimated in excess of **$40,000** require competitive basis; **$75,000+** requires full public bidding
- **§(6):** Emergency waiver requires board resolution declaring existence of emergency — defined as "a condition likely to result in immediate physical injury to persons or to property"
- **§(12):** Explicitly excludes "construction management services, value engineering, constructability review, building commissioning, and other construction-related professional and personal services"

### What the Statute Does NOT Cover

Professional staffing services — nurses, therapists, psychologists, paraprofessionals — are **not enumerated** in the statute. The district's use of the professional services exemption to avoid competitive bidding on multi-million-dollar staffing contracts is a **policy interpretation**, not a statutory mandate.

### Chief's Cleared Framing (Verbatim)

> *"State law requires competitive bidding for equipment and supplies purchases exceeding $75,000. Professional staffing contracts fall outside that mandate. Vancouver Public Schools chose not to seek competitive bids on a vendor relationship that has accumulated more than $28 million in payments over six years — a decision permitted by state policy but not required by it."*

### Policy 6220 Waiver Categories (from LanceDB)

VPS Policy 6220 allows the district to waive bid requirements for purchases:
1. Clearly and legitimately limited to a single source of supply
2. Involving special facilities or market conditions
3. In the event of an emergency
4. Of insurance or bonds

**14 text chunks** across **5 separate P6220 draft revisions** (2021, 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025) confirm this waiver language has remained consistent. The district has revised P6220 at least five times without adding competitive bidding requirements for professional staffing services.
