## PREAMBLE

Project WoodWard is preparing for publication an investigative report examining the growth of Vancouver Public Schools' payments to private staffing agencies from $349,709 in fiscal year 2020-21 to $13,326,622 in fiscal year 2024-25, and the governance and procurement mechanisms through which these payments were authorized.

All figures cited below are derived from publicly available records: VPS Board Warrant Registers obtained via the BoardDocs portal, OSPI F-195 Budget Reports, and board meeting agendas and minutes. Contract terms are quoted from the 2021 Maxim Healthcare Master Service Agreement, which was attached to a board packet in the BoardDocs system.

We are requesting responses from the district to the following questions. Your responses, in full, will be incorporated into the published report. If you decline to respond, that will be noted.

## QUESTIONS FOR VPS COMMUNICATIONS / INTERIM SUPERINTENDENT BLECHSCHMIDT

### Question 1 — Vendor Spending During Austerity

District Warrant Registers show that VPS paid $13,326,622 to private staffing vendors (Amergis Healthcare, ProCare Therapy, Soliant Health, and Maxim Healthcare) in fiscal year 2024-25 — the same year the district eliminated more than 260 positions (headcount, not FTE), citing a $35 million structural deficit.

**Can the district explain the rationale for maintaining this level of vendor expenditure during a period of significant budget reductions? Were alternatives to agency staffing formally evaluated?**

### Question 2 — Competitive Bidding

State law (RCW 28A.335.190) requires competitive bidding for equipment and supplies purchases exceeding $75,000 but does not enumerate professional staffing contracts. Board Policy 6220 was revised at least five times between 2021 and 2025 without adding competitive bidding requirements for professional staffing services.

**Did VPS at any point solicit competitive bids, request for proposals (RFPs), or request for qualifications (RFQs) for staffing services during the period when vendor payments grew from $349,709 to $13.3 million annually? If not, why not?**

### Question 3 — Contract Terms

The 2021 Maxim Healthcare Master Service Agreement contains an auto-renewal clause (Section 1.1), a 90-day termination notice requirement (Section 1.2), a 30-day unilateral rate change provision (Section 5.4), and automatic annual rate increases (Section 5.5), with no maximum annual expenditure cap.

**Does the current Amergis contract contain the same or substantially similar provisions? Has the district taken any steps to renegotiate these terms, including adding expenditure caps, competitive rebidding requirements, or sunset clauses?**

### Question 4 — Board Approval Estimate vs. Actual Spend

The July 2024 board recommendation for the Amergis contract described an "Approximate Cost to District" of $3,000,000 for the 2024-25 school year. This figure was not presented as a contractual spending cap. Actual payments to Amergis in FY24-25 totaled $10,970,973.

**What mechanism exists for the board to review, authorize, or be notified when actual vendor payments significantly exceed the cost estimate presented at the time of contract approval? Was the board informed when Amergis payments exceeded the $3 million estimate?**

### Question 5 — Consent Agenda Governance

This investigation's review of board records indicates that staffing vendor contracts were approved as Action Items (with individual discussion) in earlier years but were approved via the Consent Agenda by 2024, as the contract values had grown significantly.

**Does the district have a policy or threshold that determines when a vendor contract should be presented as a separate Action Item rather than included on the Consent Agenda? If so, what is the threshold? If no formal policy or threshold exists, who decides whether an item is placed on the Consent Agenda versus presented as a standalone Action Item — and is that decision documented anywhere (policy, procedure, or administrative guidance)?**

### Question 6 — Emergency Cash Flow

Board records document the district's use of interfund loans (Resolution #906, July 2023; Resolution #934, June 2025) and registered warrants from the Clark County Treasurer (Resolution #924, August 2024) for three consecutive fiscal years to meet cash flow needs.

**During the period when the district required emergency cash-flow mechanisms to make payroll, did administration evaluate whether reducing staffing vendor expenditures could alleviate the cash flow shortfall?**

## QUESTIONS FOR JEFF FISH, HR EXECUTIVE

### Question 7 — Recruitment Efforts

The district's payments to private staffing agencies — primarily for special education teachers, SLPs, school psychologists, behavioral specialists, nurses, and paraprofessionals — grew from $349,709 in FY20-21 to $13.3 million in FY24-25. These payments represent the cost of roles the district was unable to fill with direct-hire employees.

**What specific recruitment strategies, retention incentives, and hiring campaigns did the HR department deploy during this period to recruit and retain direct-hire staff in these high-vacancy categories? Does the department track time-to-fill metrics for these roles? Can the department provide any of the following: an annual vacancy report, a time-to-fill dashboard, a hard-to-fill incentive policy, or documentation of recruiting campaigns targeting SPED professionals?**

### Question 8 — Placement Fee Impact

The 2021 Maxim MSA contains a placement fee provision (Section 1.1) requiring the district to pay "$5,000 or 30% of annualized salary" if it directly hires an agency worker within 12 months of their placement.

**Has the district ever paid a placement fee to convert an agency worker to a direct-hire employee? Has the placement fee provision influenced the district's decisions about whether to convert agency workers to permanent positions?**

## QUESTIONS FOR BOARD CHAIR / VPS COMMUNICATIONS

### Question 9 — Board Awareness of Vendor Corporate History

Board records from the June 18, 2024 Committee of the Whole identify the vendor as "Amergis Education (formerly Maxim Healthcare)." On September 12, 2011, the U.S. Department of Justice announced that Maxim Healthcare Services Inc. entered into a resolution of criminal and civil matters related to alleged fraudulent billings to government health programs, including a deferred prosecution agreement and approximately $150 million in combined criminal and civil payments. The DOJ also reported guilty pleas by individuals in related conduct. *(Source: U.S. Department of Justice, Office of Public Affairs, Press Release, September 12, 2011)*

This investigation intends to cite the DOJ settlement in a published report as context for vendor due diligence practices. If the district believes any factual detail above is inaccurate, we invite correction at this stage.

**Was the board formally briefed on Maxim Healthcare's 2011 DOJ settlement at any point during the vendor relationship? If so, when and in what form? Does the district maintain a vendor due diligence process that includes review of corporate legal history for contractors receiving payments of this magnitude?**

### Question 10 — Vendor Concentration

A single corporate entity (Maxim Healthcare / Amergis Healthcare) has received $28,074,510 in payments from VPS over five fiscal years — 87.2% of all staffing vendor payments.

**Does the board consider this level of vendor concentration consistent with sound fiscal stewardship? Has the board at any point directed administration to diversify its staffing vendor relationships or conduct a competitive procurement process for these services?**

### Question 11 — Spending Authority

VPS Board Policy 6210 (Purchasing: Authorization and Control) requires the board to annually designate district employees authorized to enter into and sign contracts on behalf of the district.

**Does the district's P6210 spending authority delegation include a dollar threshold above which contracts or cumulative vendor payments require separate board authorization? If so, was that threshold triggered by the cumulative $28 million in staffing vendor payments? If no such threshold exists, does the board believe one should be established?**
