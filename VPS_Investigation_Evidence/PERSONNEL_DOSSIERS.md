## 1. Brett Blechschmidt

**Current Title/Role:** Interim Superintendent, Vancouver Public Schools (appointed May 2025; agreed not to seek permanent position per Board/District announcement, May 13, 2025)
**Organization:** Vancouver Public Schools, District #37
**Relevance to Investigation:** Primary budget architect during the austerity period. Presented the $35 million reduction package to the board on March 12, 2024. Oversaw fiscal services and purchasing departments during the period of $32M+ staffing vendor payments. Now holds authority over both the district budget AND the organizational response to questions about the budget.

**Compensation (Public Record):**
- 2024-25 S-275 Salary: ~$254,727 (Executive tier, Root 11-13)
- Verify exact figures year-by-year at fiscal.wa.gov

**Career History:**
- Interim Superintendent, VPS (May 2025–present)
- Associate Superintendent / Chief Operating Officer / Chief Financial Officer, VPS (~11 years)
- Educational Service District 112 (~19 years)
- Total career: ~30 years in education administration

**Public Record:**
- Presented the $35M reduction package at the March 12, 2024 board meeting (The Columbian, March 13, 2024)
- Under his financial stewardship, fund balance declined from ~$7 million to $195,180
- Two SAO audit findings (2021 and 2024) broke a 13-year clean audit streak
- Spouse: Jennifer Blechschmidt, Principal at Eisenhower Elementary within VPS (WASA awards recipient)

**Key Investigation Findings:**
- Architect of the budget that cut 217 FTEs while preserving executive compensation pools
- Oversaw purchasing department (David Hurt) that processed multi-million-dollar vendor contracts without competitive bidding
- Framed Central Office as taking "the highest percentage" of cuts — a technically accurate but asymmetrically impacting characterization (S-275 data shows most Central Office reductions were $48K support staff, not $183K executives)

**Public Records Available:**
- VPS board meeting records where he presented
- Columbian quotes/interviews
- ESD 112 personnel history
- S-275 salary data at fiscal.wa.gov (all years 2020–2025)

## 2. Jeff Fish

**Current Title/Role:** Executive Director of Human Resources, VPS (since spring 2022); also serves as Title IX Coordinator
**Organization:** Vancouver Public Schools
**Relevance to Investigation:** His department's recruitment outcomes are central to the investigation: every unfilled position becomes an additional billing opportunity for staffing vendors. Oversees a 5-person HR department responsible for recruiting a ~2,500-person workforce.

**Compensation (Public Record):**
- 2024-25 S-275 Salary: ~$217,873 (Executive tier, Root 11-13)

**Career History:**
- Executive Director of Human Resources, VPS (July 2022–present)
- Director of HR, Clackamas Education Service District (May 2017–June 2022)
- Senior Legal Counsel for Labor and Employment / Director of Performance Management, Portland Public Schools
- HR Director, Portland Development Commission
- Legal Aid Services of Oregon
- Education: JD, Catholic University of America; BA, College of William and Mary

**Public Record:**
- One of few district HR directors in the region with formal legal training
- Remarkably low public profile: this investigation found **zero media quotes, zero attributed board presentations, zero public statements** across 4 years of local news coverage in The Columbian, Clark County Today, KATU, or OPB
- Coverage consistently attributes staffing and bargaining commentary to Jessica Roberts (Communications) or the superintendent — never Fish

**Key Investigation Findings:**
- Hired in 2022 as vendor payments were accelerating from $2.1M to $6.2M annually
- HR department's recruitment failures directly correlate with escalating vendor dependence
- No public evidence of any strategic workforce pipeline initiative during his tenure

**Public Records Available:**

- Washington PERC filings involving VPS

- Oregon Employment Relations Board filings from PPS/Clackamas tenure

## 11. Larry Roe

**Current Title/Role:** Independent Community Critic / Analyst
**Organization:** Community member; PhD chemist, retired Intel engineering manager
**Relevance to Investigation:** Published detailed financial analyses providing contemporary counter-narratives to VPS's financial presentations.

**Public Record:**
- Ran for VPS board in 2023 and 2025 (lost both times)
- The Columbian editorial board acknowledged him as "a strong candidate" even while endorsing his opponent
- Published in Clark County Today documenting:
  - VPS total employee count grew from 2,225 (2014) to 3,300 (2024) — 48.3% increase — while enrollment dropped by 1,000+ students
  - Spending exceeded tax receipts by $9M, $15M, and $7M in three consecutive years
  - Per-student spending doubled over 10 years while test scores declined
  - Property taxes rose ~254.8% from 2014 to 2024
  - $8.5M Capital Projects Fund transfer to General Fund in 2023-24

**Public Records Available:**
- Clark County Today articles by/about Roe
