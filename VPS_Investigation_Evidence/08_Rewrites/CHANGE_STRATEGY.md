# Change Strategy

If the goal is real accountability rather than simply publication, the work should now move on three tracks at the same time: journalism, oversight, and public pressure.

## 1. Journalism track: make it easy for a reporter to say yes

Do not lead with the full evidence package.

Lead with one sentence and three attachments.

Recommended lead:

Vancouver Public Schools cut more than 260 positions during a declared fiscal emergency, then paid $13.3 million in one year to private staffing vendors, including $10.97 million to Amergis, the rebranded successor to Maxim Healthcare, under a contract structure with no public spending cap.

The three attachments should be:

- the one-page executive summary,
- a canonical figures sheet,
- and a short list of source documents a reporter can verify quickly.

Do not send all four original articles first. Send the shorter, sharper rewrites or even just Article 1 and Article 2 from this folder.

If Kimberly Cortez is still the warmest lead, give her the story, not the package. The posture should be: these are my verified records and calculations, you can own the reporting from here.

## 2. Oversight track: route the findings to institutions that can act without publishing a story

The strongest non-journalism channel in the handoffs material is the Washington State Auditor's Office.

The most actionable oversight referral is not a general complaint that the district is troubled. It is a tightly framed submission alleging a breakdown in financial controls and board visibility around a large, uncapped vendor relationship during a period of emergency borrowing and reserve depletion.

The submission should focus on verifiable questions such as:

- whether a vendor relationship that grew to $13.3 million in one year received adequate internal control and board-level review,
- whether the variance between the $3 million board estimate and the $10.97 million paid to Amergis triggered any internal escalation,
- whether procurement and contract-management controls were adequate for auto-renewing professional-services agreements with no spending ceiling,
- and whether repeated emergency borrowing should have triggered stronger oversight of Object 7 variance drivers.

That route does not depend on a newsroom deciding the story is timely.

## 3. Public-records track: create requests that other people can file easily

The next records requests should be narrow, high-yield, and easy for a journalist or outside watchdog to understand.

Recommended priority requests:

1. The full Amergis master service agreement and all amendments, addenda, and rate sheets in effect for 2024-25 and 2025-26.
2. Any internal variance analysis, board briefing, memo, email summary, or finance report discussing the difference between the $3 million Amergis estimate and the actual amount paid.
3. Any vendor-spend or purchased-services dashboard, monthly finance packet, or internal report breaking down Object 7 spending by vendor or subcategory for 2023-24 through 2025-26.
4. Any document identifying who approved, reviewed, renewed, or monitored Amergis and Maxim staffing agreements, including routing sheets or signature approvals.
5. Any recruitment and vacancy analysis for special education teachers, paras, SLPs, psychologists, nurses, and behavior staff from 2022 forward, including time-to-fill, vacancy counts, and exit summaries.
6. Any conversion analysis discussing hiring agency workers into district employment, including use of placement-fee provisions.
7. Any board or executive briefing regarding registered warrants, emergency advances, or Object 7 overages that mentions staffing vendors.

These requests are designed so a reporter, civic watchdog, or outside resident could file them without needing your full backstory.

## 4. Public-pressure track: force a narrow question into the open

The most effective public pressure is likely to come from a small number of simple, repeatable questions, not from dropping the whole theory of the case at once.

The three strongest public questions are:

1. Why did the board approve an Amergis item listed at approximately $3 million when actual payments reached $10.97 million?
2. What is the district's public plan to reduce contractor dependence and convert recurring agency labor into permanent hiring?
3. How much of the FY24-25 Object 7 overage was driven by staffing vendors?

Those questions can be repeated by journalists, union members, parents, watchdog groups, and board-meeting commenters without needing to master the entire evidence package.

## 5. Best immediate sequence

1. Tighten the reporter package down to a one-page pitch plus a few documents.
2. Prepare a short SAO-facing oversight memo using the control-and-variance framing above.
3. Hand Kimberly or another reporter a ready-to-file records request list rather than asking them to read everything first.
4. Build a public tracker showing what has been requested, what has been answered, and what remains missing.
5. Keep the broader investigation package intact as the source archive behind those narrower moves.

## Bottom line

The work was not wasted. It is simply overbuilt for the first contact.

Right now the highest-leverage move is not writing a fifth long article. It is breaking the project into smaller weapons: one story hook for reporters, one oversight referral for auditors, and one simple records list that other people can use without exposing you.
