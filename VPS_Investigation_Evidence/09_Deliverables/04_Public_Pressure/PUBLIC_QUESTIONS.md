# Three Questions for the Vancouver Public Schools Board of Directors

These questions can be asked at board meetings, in letters to the editor, in union communications, by parent groups, or by any member of the public. They require no specialized knowledge to ask and no background in the full investigation to understand.

---

## Question 1: The $3 Million Estimate

**On July 9, 2024, the board approved an Amergis staffing contract listed at approximately $3 million. Actual payments to Amergis that year reached $10.97 million. What happened?**

Specifically:

- Was the board informed when spending exceeded the approved estimate?
- Who authorized the additional $7.97 million?
- What internal control was supposed to catch this variance, and did it?

*Source: Board Minutes, July 9, 2024 (Consent Agenda Item #9) vs. FY2024-25 Warrant Registers, both available on VPS BoardDocs.*

---

## Question 2: The Contractor-to-Hire Plan

**What is the district's public plan to reduce staffing vendor dependence and convert recurring agency labor into permanent positions?**

Specifically:

- Has the district conducted a cost comparison between vendor rates and direct-hire costs?
- Has the district evaluated the 30% conversion fee in the Amergis contract and its effect on hiring?
- What is the target for reducing staffing vendor spending in FY2026-27?

*Context: VPS paid $13.3 million to staffing vendors in FY2024-25. At the SPED teacher midpoint, vendor rates run 23% above the district's fully loaded cost for a direct hire.*

---

## Question 3: What Drove the Overage

**How much of the FY2024-25 Object 7 (Purchased Services) budget overage was driven by staffing vendors?**

Specifically:

- Object 7 spending exceeded the budget by $10.6 million. How much of that overage is attributable to staffing agency payments?
- Does the district produce a vendor-level breakdown of Object 7, and is it shared with the board?
- When district spending on outside services runs above budget, how and when is the board told?

*Source: OSPI F-195 Budget Reports for VPS ($36.7M budgeted, $47.3M actual).* 

---

## How to Use These Questions

- **At a board meeting:** Read one question during public comment. Keep it to 60 seconds. Ask for a written response.
- **In a letter to the editor:** Frame one question as the lead. The Columbian, Clark County Today, and OPB all cover VPS.
- **To a reporter:** Hand them the question and the source citation. Let them verify and ask it themselves.
- **To a union rep:** VEA and VAESP leadership can raise these through collective bargaining, grievance procedures, or public testimony.
- **To incoming leadership:** Dr. Torres-Morales starts July 1, 2026. These questions define what he inherits.

---

## What These Questions Are Not

These are not allegations of fraud or corruption. They are basic governance questions that any taxpayer, parent, or staff member has the right to ask and the board has the obligation to answer.

The documents needed to answer them are either already public or obtainable through a standard records request under RCW 42.56.
