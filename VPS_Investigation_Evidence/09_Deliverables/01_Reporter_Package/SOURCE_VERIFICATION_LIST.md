# Source Verification List

These are the main documents you can use to verify the core claims in this package.

This list is organized in two tiers:

1. A quick-confidence path to test the story without doing a deep audit.
2. A deeper-document path for when you want to keep pushing.

Each source below is either publicly accessible without a records request or directly obtainable through a standard Washington public-records request.

---

## First-Pass Check: Verify The Main Hook

Start with these three. Together, they let you test the core contradiction in the package.

### 1. Board Minutes,July 9, 2024

**What it proves:** The board approved the Amergis contract at "approximately $3,000,000" on the consent agenda.

**Where to find it:** VPS BoardDocs portal → Board of Directors → Meeting Minutes → July 9, 2024 → Consent Agenda, Item #9.

**What to look for:** The Amergis Education line item and the listed dollar amount.

**Why this matters:** This is the board-facing figure used as the package's starting benchmark.

### 2. Warrant Registers,FY2024-25

**What it helps you verify:** The package's $10,970,973 Amergis figure is tied to the district's monthly payment records.

**Where to find it:** VPS BoardDocs portal → Board of Directors → Meeting documents → Monthly warrant register attachments (September 2024 through August 2025). Search for "AMERGIS HEALTHCARE STAFFING INC."

**What to do in a first pass:** Do not try to rebuild the full yearly total right away. Instead, confirm that the package identifies monthly warrant registers as the underlying payment trail and that Amergis appears in those records.

**If you want to spot-check one month yourself:** Pick any single month during FY2024-25. Go to VPS BoardDocs, find that month's warrant register, and search for "AMERGIS HEALTHCARE STAFFING INC." Add up the Amergis amounts you find. Then open the package's individual payment records file (01_Payment_Data/vendor_payments_all_records.csv), filter to Amergis entries with dates in that same month, and compare your total to the package's records. If the numbers match, the compilation method is working. You do not need to do this for every month. One match builds confidence in the assembly process.

**If you want to go deeper later:** The package's compiled FY2024-25 Amergis total is $10,970,973. A full month-by-month reconstruction is possible, but it is optional deeper audit work, not a first-hour requirement.

**Why this matters:** This is the actual-payment side of the story.

### 3. DOJ Press Release,October 7, 2011

**What it proves:** Maxim Healthcare Services entered a federal criminal guilty plea and paid $150 million to resolve fraud charges.

**Where to find it:** Search "DOJ Maxim Healthcare 2011 settlement" or access via the Department of Justice press release archive, October 7, 2011.

**Why this matters:** This source is included as a due-diligence and governance point, not as proof of current misconduct in this case.

## Second-Pass Check: Confirm The Broader Fiscal Context

Once the main hook feels real, these documents help answer the next question: what was happening in the district's broader finances at the same time?

### 4. OSPI F-195 Budget Report,VPS FY2024-25

**What it proves:** Object 7 was budgeted at $36.7M but actual spending reached $47.3M,a $10.6M overage. VPS budgets Object 7 at 8.67% of General Fund, lower than peer districts.

**Where to find it:** OSPI website → School Apportionment and Financial Services → F-195 reports → Vancouver School District #37, FY2024-25.

**What `Object 7` means in plain English:** purchased services, meaning money paid to outside entities rather than regular district payroll staff.

**Why this matters:** It shows that outside-services spending overall also ran above budget.

### 5. OSPI S-275 Personnel Data

**What it proves:** Staffing levels, salary tiers, and where cuts actually fell. Total FTE dropped from 2,736.83 to 2,519.90 (-216.93). Executive tier compensation pool contracted 6.89%; support staff bore the largest absolute reductions.

**Where to find it:** fiscal.wa.gov → S-275 Personnel Reporting → Vancouver School District #37, FY2023-24 and FY2024-25.

**Why this matters:** This is one of the sources used to test whether staffing reductions and outside-vendor dependence were happening at the same time.

### 6. The Columbian,March 13, 2024

**What it proves:** The board voted unanimously on the $35 million reduction package. Contemporaneous reporting includes direct quotes from staff testimony.

**Where to find it:** The Columbian archives, search "Vancouver Public Schools $35 million cuts."

**Why this matters:** This helps anchor the public narrative of austerity that the payment story is being compared against.

## Deeper Document Review: Contract Structure And Oversight

These are the documents to use once you want to understand the contract terms and governance structure more fully.

### 7. Maxim/Amergis Master Service Agreement (May 2021)

**What it proves:** Auto-renewal, uncapped rate increases, no spending cap, 30% conversion fee.

**Where to find it:** Included in this package ([maxim_master_agreement_and_attachment_a_2021.pdf](maxim_master_agreement_and_attachment_a_2021.pdf)). You can also request the agreement directly from VPS under RCW 42.56.

**Why this matters:** This is the document behind the package's argument that the district was operating inside a contract structure with weak spending restraints.

### 8. VPS Board Policy 6220,Bid or Request for Proposal Requirements

**What it proves:** Five revisions during the spending growth period; none added staffing-specific oversight.

**Where to find it:** VPS BoardDocs → Board Policies → Policy 6220. Historical versions in this package (05_Source_Documents/Policy_6220_2021_Draft.pdf, Policy_6220_2024_Draft.pdf).

**Why this matters:** This gives you the policy backdrop for questions about procurement, oversight, and whether staffing contracts received meaningful board scrutiny.

## Additional Context: Peer And State-Level Comparison

These sources are useful once you want to test whether VPS looks unusual compared with nearby districts or state-level financial oversight.

### 9. OSPI F-195,Evergreen SD and Battle Ground SD

**What it proves:** Peer Clark County districts budget Object 7 at 10.89% (Evergreen) and 13.49% (Battle Ground) of General Fund,compared to VPS at 8.67%. VPS underbudgets then overspends.

**Where to find it:** OSPI F-195 reports for Evergreen SD #114 and Battle Ground SD #119, FY2024-25.

**Why this matters:** This gives a rough local benchmark. It helps show that VPS was not simply budgeting at the same outside-services level as its peers.

### 10. OSPI Apportionment Advance Confirmation

**What it proves:** VPS requested $21.4M in emergency borrowing for FY2025-26; OSPI approved $8.7M.

**Where to find it:** Confirmed in writing by Shawn Lewis, Director of School Apportionment and Financial Services, OSPI (March 2, 2026 email on file). OSPI can confirm independently.

**Why this matters:** This helps show that the district's financial stress was not theoretical.
