## The Story in Plain English

In March 2024, Vancouver Public Schools told the public it had to make painful cuts because of a budget crisis. The board approved more than 260 position cuts.

In that same fiscal year, the district's own records show $13.3 million in payments to outside staffing vendors. Of that amount, $10.97 million went to Amergis Healthcare Staffing.

Board materials described the Amergis contract at approximately $3 million. The district's payment records show nearly $11 million paid to Amergis during that fiscal year.

That gap does not prove fraud or corruption. It does create a clear reporting question: what did the board think it was approving, what did the district actually spend, and how was that spending monitored during a declared fiscal emergency?

Everything in this package is built from public records, public reporting, or calculations based on public records.

## Key Terms in Ordinary Language

### BoardDocs

The online portal where school-board meeting agendas, minutes, and attachments are posted.

### Warrant Register

A district payment record showing money paid out to named vendors. In plain language, this is part of the district's paper trail for who got paid and how much.

### Consent Agenda

A block of items the board approves together, usually without separate discussion of each item.

### Object 7

Washington school-finance shorthand for purchased services. In plain language, it is the bucket for money paid to outside entities rather than regular payroll employees.

### F-195

Washington State's school-district budget report. In this package, it is used to show what the district budgeted and what it later reported as actual spending.

### S-275

Washington State's personnel-reporting system. In this package, it is used to show staffing levels, salary tiers, and where workforce changes appeared.

### Staffing Vendor

An outside company that supplies workers to the district. In this package, that can include special education staff, therapists, nurses, and related contracted roles.

### Right of Reply

A request for comment sent to people or institutions named in a story so they have a chance to respond before publication.

### SEBB

School Employees Benefits Board. Washington State's mandatory health benefit program for school district employees. In this package, the monthly SEBB rate is used to calculate the fully loaded cost of a direct-hire employee.

### DRS

Department of Retirement Systems. Washington State's pension program. In this package, the employer contribution rate (9.11%) is used in the cost model comparing direct-hire costs to vendor rates.

### Public Records Request

A formal request for government documents under Washington's Public Records Act.

## What Is Still Open

- How the district moved from an approximate $3 million public figure to nearly $11 million in Amergis payments during the fiscal year.
- Which internal officials approved, monitored, or explained that growth.
- Whether the board received a fuller spending picture than what was visible in public-facing approval materials.
- What records exist showing variance analysis, vendor-level monitoring, or approval of rate changes.
- What district officials say now about the spending, the controls, and the staffing strategy.
