# Missing Data and How to Get It

This document shows what the package already documents, what is still missing, why the missing piece matters, and the most practical next step to get it.

This is not a list of weaknesses in the story. It is a reporting map.

Each item below answers five questions: what is still missing, why it matters, how important it is right now, where to get it, and what to do next. Start with the items marked `Highest priority`.

---

## Highest-Priority Gaps

### 1. What explains the gap between the board's approximate $3 million Amergis figure and the package's $10.97 million FY2024-25 total?

**Why it matters:** This is the clearest governance question in the package. If records exist showing variance analysis, budget amendments, internal warnings, or board briefings, they could reveal whether district officials tracked and explained the gap. If no such records exist, that also matters.

**Why it is high priority:** This is the fastest path from the existing package into new reporting.

**Most likely sources:** Internal finance memos, variance analyses, budget-amendment documents, board briefings, executive emails.

**Best next step:** File Request 2 in [PUBLIC_RECORDS_REQUESTS.md](../03_Records_Requests/PUBLIC_RECORDS_REQUESTS.md). Pair it with a comment request asking the district, in plain language, how the board-facing estimate relates to the district's actual Amergis payments.

### 2. What did the full Amergis/Maxim contract look like during FY2024-25 and FY2025-26?

**Why it matters:** The package already uses the 2021 agreement as evidence of auto-renewal, rate increases, and a conversion fee. But if there were later amendments, current rate sheets, or changed terms, those could materially affect the story.

**Why it is high priority:** A current amendment or updated rate sheet could confirm, narrow, or complicate parts of the contract story.

**Most likely sources:** Contract amendments, addenda, attachments, scope-of-work documents, rate sheets.

**Best next step:** File Request 1 in [PUBLIC_RECORDS_REQUESTS.md](../03_Records_Requests/PUBLIC_RECORDS_REQUESTS.md).

### 3. Who inside the district approved, renewed, monitored, or accepted rate changes under the Amergis/Maxim agreement?

**Why it matters:** The story becomes much stronger if you can identify the approval chain rather than describing the process abstractly.

**Why it is high priority:** This is where the governance question becomes personal and specific.

**Most likely sources:** Routing sheets, approval forms, signature pages, delegated-authority documents, renewal records, purchasing approvals.

**Best next step:** File Request 4 in [PUBLIC_RECORDS_REQUESTS.md](../03_Records_Requests/PUBLIC_RECORDS_REQUESTS.md). If records identify officials, follow with direct comment requests.

### 4. Did the district internally track vendor-level purchased-services spending and discuss it with the board?

**Why it matters:** If the district had vendor-level dashboards or finance packets, those records could show whether Amergis spending was visible internally in a way the public did not see.

**Why it is high priority:** This is the best way to test whether the board and district leadership were monitoring the story the package is describing.

**Most likely sources:** Purchased-services dashboards, vendor-spend reports, internal finance packets, board briefings.

**Best next step:** File Request 3 in [PUBLIC_RECORDS_REQUESTS.md](../03_Records_Requests/PUBLIC_RECORDS_REQUESTS.md).

## High-Value Expansion Gaps

### 5. What was the district's actual recruitment and vacancy picture for special education and related roles?

**Why it matters:** The district's likely defense is that vendor dependence reflects labor-market difficulty, not preference. You will want real records showing vacancies, time-to-fill, retention problems, or failed recruitment.

**Most likely sources:** Vacancy reports, recruitment analyses, workforce-planning documents, time-to-fill reports, exit summaries.

**Best next step:** File Request 5 in [PUBLIC_RECORDS_REQUESTS.md](../03_Records_Requests/PUBLIC_RECORDS_REQUESTS.md). Then ask the district and HR officials whether those records accurately reflect staffing conditions.

### 6. Did the district ever evaluate moving agency workers into direct employment or compare vendor costs to direct-hire costs internally?

**Why it matters:** The package includes a cost-premium model. The next reporting question is whether district officials did any version of that analysis themselves.

**Most likely sources:** Cost-benefit analyses, staffing-strategy memos, conversion analyses, discussions of placement-fee provisions.

**Best next step:** File Request 6 in [PUBLIC_RECORDS_REQUESTS.md](../03_Records_Requests/PUBLIC_RECORDS_REQUESTS.md).

### 7. Was outside-services overspending discussed in connection with emergency borrowing or cash-flow stress?

**Why it matters:** The package already documents both large vendor spending and emergency borrowing. The missing link is whether district leaders discussed a connection between them.

**Most likely sources:** Finance memos, executive briefings, borrowing discussions, cash-flow analysis, board presentations.

**Best next step:** File Request 7 in [PUBLIC_RECORDS_REQUESTS.md](../03_Records_Requests/PUBLIC_RECORDS_REQUESTS.md).

## Useful But Not Immediate Gaps

### 8. Was there a fuller public explanation of the Amergis/Maxim continuity than what appears in the current package?

**Why it matters:** The package already treats Amergis as the renamed staffing division of Maxim. The next question is whether district officials ever publicly explained that continuity in a way the board or public would have understood.

**Most likely sources:** Board presentations, meeting attachments, committee packets, communications records.

**Best next step:** Check board materials more broadly and include this question in comment requests to district officials.

### 9. How much of the package's classroom-impact story can be supported by district-specific records rather than external reporting?

**Why it matters:** The money-trail story is stronger than the classroom-impact story because the classroom section relies more heavily on outside reporting and complaint records. A journalist may want district-specific records on service delivery, restraint, staffing continuity, or training.

**Most likely sources:** Complaint decisions, service-delivery reports, restraint reports, training records, staffing logs.

**Best next step:** Treat this as a second-stage reporting lane after the core money/governance story is established.

### 10. Can the district's full staffing-vendor picture be expanded beyond the core staffing-vendor total without muddying the central story?

**Why it matters:** The package is transparent that some vendors, including Aveanna and Stepping Stones, are tracked separately. You may eventually want the broader vendor universe, but expanding too early could blur the central narrative.

**Most likely sources:** Existing payment summaries, additional warrant-record review, follow-up records requests if needed.

**Best next step:** Treat this as optional expansion work, not as a first-wave requirement.

4. Decide whether the story is primarily about the spending gap, the contract structure, or the broader oversight culture.
5. Only then expand into recruitment, conversion, and classroom-impact lanes.

## The Main Principle

The strongest version of this reporting does not require every gap to be closed before the story becomes real.

The central question is already visible in public records. These missing pieces are the next places to press if you want to strengthen, deepen, or personalize the story.
