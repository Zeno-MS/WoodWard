# Story Summary: VPS Paid $13.3 Million to Staffing Vendors While Cutting 260+ Positions

Vancouver Public Schools cut more than 260 positions during a declared fiscal emergency, then paid $13.3 million in one year to private staffing vendors, including $10.97 million to Amergis, the rebranded successor to Maxim Healthcare, under a contract structure with no public spending cap.

---

## The core contradiction

On March 12, 2024, the VPS Board of Directors voted unanimously to cut $35 million in spending, eliminating roughly 260 positions. The same fiscal year, the district paid $13.3 million to private staffing agencies to fill roles it had just eliminated or failed to recruit for, more than it had ever spent on contractors in a single year.

## The contract

The dominant vendor relationship is governed by a Master Service Agreement signed in May 2021 with Maxim Healthcare (now operating as Amergis). Key terms:

- **Auto-renewal** annually unless 90-day notice is given
- **Uncapped rate increases** on 30-day notice with no ceiling
- **No spending cap**, no maximum annual expenditure
- **30% conversion fee** penalizing the district for directly hiring any worker placed by the vendor
- **No competitive bidding** was ever conducted

On July 9, 2024, the board approved this contract on the consent agenda alongside a school mascot vote and a software purchase. The stated cost: "approximately $3,000,000." Actual payments that year: $10,970,973.

## The vendor

Maxim Healthcare Services entered a federal criminal guilty plea in 2011 and paid $150 million to resolve DOJ charges of systematic Medicaid fraud. VPS signed its current Master Service Agreement with Maxim ten years later. Combined, Maxim and its rebranded successor Amergis captured 87.2% of all staffing vendor payments, $28 million of $32.2 million over five years.

## The fiscal context

VPS is the only Clark County school district to receive a state financial warning. It has required emergency borrowing for three consecutive years. Its fund balance dropped from $7 million to $195,180. The Clark County Treasurer publicly warned that "county treasurers are not designed to provide ongoing operating loans to school districts."
