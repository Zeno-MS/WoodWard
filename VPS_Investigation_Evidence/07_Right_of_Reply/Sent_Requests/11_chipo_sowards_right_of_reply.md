Hello VAESP President, 

Please forward this message to VAESP leadership and the VAESP bargaining team. If VAESP prefers to respond from a union email address, that is fine.

I am finalizing a report on Special Education staffing stability in Vancouver Public Schools, with a focus on paraeducator retention and training, and the use of contracted classroom support staff.

A key finding from the board document corpus is that Brett Blechschmidt presented macro budgets in the mandatory summer budget hearings, including Object 7 “Purchased Services” totals. Those packets show the totals. What is missing is any public record of role-level cost models that explain contractor premiums, including paraeducator coverage, or any documented board briefing on contractor onboarding and de-escalation training standards for classroom staff.

Please respond in writing by 5:00 PM Pacific on Friday, March 6, 2026. I am not asking for student-identifying information. If you can point to public documentation, bargaining proposals, grievance themes, member surveys, or written communications from the district, that helps.

During the mandatory summer budget hearings in 2022, 2023, and 2024, Mr. Blechschmidt presented budgets with large “Purchased Services” totals. On what date, if any, did the district provide VAESP a role-level cost model explaining paraeducator staffing premiums paid through vendors?

If VAESP was provided a role-level model, what did it show about unit costs, the premium compared to internal para wages, and the projected growth?

If VAESP was not provided that model, did VAESP request it? What was the district’s response and who responded?

Retention. What did VAESP ask the district to do to retain paraeducators in SPED settings over the last two school years? What was accepted, rejected, or left unfunded?

Training. What training does VAESP consider non-negotiable for paras in high-need classrooms? De-escalation, crisis response, safety procedures, documentation expectations, confidentiality, mandatory reporting. Which elements are consistently provided now and which are not?

Contractor classroom staffing. When contractor paraeducators or contractor classroom support staff are used, what training should be required before placement? Who should verify completion and where should that verification live?

Has the district ever provided VAESP a written training standard for contractor paras? If yes, please identify it. If no, did VAESP request it?

Supervision. In practice, who is responsible when a para is placed without adequate training or support? Building administration, Special Services leadership, HR, vendor, or shared? What does VAESP believe the accountability chain should be?

Safety and injuries. Are injury risk and lack of staffing coverage driving retention problems right now? What fixes has VAESP proposed and what response has the district given?

Role substitution concern. Without making claims about intent, are members seeing patterns where para vacancies remain open while alternative coverage expands? If yes, what would need to be tracked to measure it rather than argue about anecdotes?

SB 5263. With increased state special education funding, what would VAESP want formalized in writing to stabilize para staffing and reduce vendor dependence?

For the incoming superintendent and Board, what three requirements should be put in writing so para retention and training are treated as a governance requirement?

Thank you for your time.

Regards
