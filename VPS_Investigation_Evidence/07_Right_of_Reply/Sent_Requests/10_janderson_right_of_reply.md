Hello VEA leadership,

I am finalizing a report on Special Education staffing stability and contracted staffing in Vancouver Public Schools. The focus is governance and working conditions. Retention of SPED teachers and paras, training and supervision of contracted classroom staff, and what decision-makers and labor were told as “Purchased Services” accelerated.

A key finding from the board document corpus is that Brett Blechschmidt presented macro budgets in the mandatory summer budget hearings, including Object 7 “Purchased Services” totals. Board packets include high-level totals. What is missing is any public record of a role-level cost model or vendor premium analysis that explains why Purchased Services accelerated so quickly or what share was contractor classroom staffing.

Please respond in writing by 5:00 PM Pacific on Friday, March 6, 2026. I am not asking for student-identifying information. If you can point to public documentation, bargaining proposals, member surveys, grievance themes, or written communications from the district, that helps.

During the mandatory summer budget hearings in 2022, 2023, and 2024, Mr. Blechschmidt presented budgets that included large “Purchased Services” totals. On what date, if any, did the district provide VEA a role-level cost model that breaks down contractor premiums for hard-to-fill roles, especially contractor teachers and paras?

If VEA was provided a role-level model, what did it show about unit costs, the premium compared to internal CBA costs, and projected year-over-year growth?

If VEA was not provided that model, did VEA request it? What was the district’s response, and who responded?

Role-level visibility. Did the district provide any role-level reporting to VEA for contracted staffing by category (contract teacher, paraeducator, SLP, nurse, psychologist) with hours and cost? If yes, what was the report called? If no, what was offered instead?

Retention. What did VEA ask the district to do to retain SPED teachers over the last two school years? What was accepted, rejected, or left unfunded?

Exit intelligence. Does VEA have member survey data or exit interview themes specific to SPED teacher departures or transfers? What are the recurring drivers?

Contractor training. Has the district provided VEA a written training standard for contractor teachers working in SPED settings? Topics like classroom management, de-escalation, documentation, confidentiality, and safety procedures. If yes, please identify what exists. If no, did VEA request it?

Supervision and accountability. In members’ experience, who supervises contractor teachers day to day in SPED settings? Building administration, Special Services, vendor supervisor, or shared? Where does accountability break down?

Substitution concern. Without speculating about intent, are members reporting patterns where permanent roles stayed vacant or became harder to fill while contractor coverage expanded? If yes, what patterns are you hearing and what documentation exists in bargaining, grievances, or staffing reports?

Placement fees. Vendor contracts include conversion penalties for hiring an agency worker into district employment. Has VEA seen these terms affect whether strong contractors can become permanent hires? If so, what patterns are members reporting?

SB 5263. With increased state special education funding, what would VEA want formalized in writing to build internal capacity and reduce vendor dependence?

For the incoming superintendent and Board, what three requirements should be formalized in writing so this does not repeat next year?

Thank you for your time.
