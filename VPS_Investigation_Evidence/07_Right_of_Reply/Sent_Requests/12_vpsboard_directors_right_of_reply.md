Hello Board Members,

I am finalizing a report based on board materials, board packet contracts, warrant registers, and budget documents regarding contracted staffing and Special Services oversight in Vancouver Public Schools.

This is a right-of-reply request. Please respond in writing by 5:00 PM Pacific on Friday, March 6, 2026. If any premise below is inaccurate, please correct it and cite the record you believe controls. If you were not briefed on an item, it is helpful to say so directly. Please do not include student-identifying information. If I do not receive a response, I will note that no response was provided.

Context for these questions: The district’s current interim superintendent, Brett Blechschmidt, served in senior finance and operations roles for roughly a decade before his interim appointment. He was the primary budget presenter for years, and board resolutions have listed him as holding contracting authority in his COO role. I am trying to understand what the Board was told, what controls the Board had, and what information did or did not reach you.

During Mr. Blechschmidt’s tenure as CFO and later as COO, what regular reporting did administration provide to the Board on Object 7 growth and staffing-vendor payments? Was this presented as a standing risk or treated as routine operations?

Please confirm whether the Board received any annual or quarterly breakdown showing what contracted staffing dollars were buying by role category. For example contractor teachers, paraeducators, SLPs, nurses, psychologists. If yes, what was the report called and how often was it delivered?

Warrant registers show bulk vendor payments and do not itemize roles. Was the Board informed that the public ledger cannot show teacher versus para versus clinician spend? If yes, what internal dashboard or report did administration provide to compensate for that blind spot?

Contracting authority. Board resolutions have listed Mr. Blechschmidt as a designated contract signer during his COO period. What were the boundaries of that delegated authority, and what oversight controls existed so the Board could see what was executed under delegated signature?

Consent agenda placement. Over time, staffing-vendor approvals appear in ways that reduce visibility, including consent agenda placement. What criteria does the Board use to decide that a high-dollar professional services item should be pulled for separate discussion? If there is no written criterion, who decides placement and what information do you receive to evaluate it?

Variance controls. When a staffing vendor contract is approved with an “approximate cost” estimate, what controls exist for the Board to be notified if year-end spending materially exceeds that estimate? Were you notified of any material variances in FY 2024 to 2025? If yes, when and how?

Training requirements for contractor classroom staff. A review of board materials did not surface a Board-level briefing that sets training and onboarding standards for contractor SPED teachers and contractor paraeducators in high-need classrooms. Were you ever briefed on required training for de-escalation, classroom management, safety procedures, and documentation standards for contractors? If yes, when and by whom?

Retention plan visibility. Was the Board ever presented with a specific retention plan for SPED teachers and paraeducators that included measurable goals, vacancy duration or time-to-fill metrics, turnover trends, or exit interview themes? If yes, when and by whom?

If the Board was not provided a retention plan and training standards for contractor classroom staff, do you believe the Board should require those as a condition of approving or renewing staffing-vendor contracts?

Competitive process. Did the Board ever direct administration to run a competitive process, structured vendor comparison, or RFP for staffing services during the period vendor dependence grew? If not, what explanation did administration provide for remaining non-competitive?

Concentration risk. Was the Board ever briefed on vendor concentration over time as a risk factor, including continuity risk, rate leverage, and quality control? If yes, when?

Conversion barriers. Board packet contracts include conversion penalties (placement fees) for hiring an agency worker into district employment. Was the Board briefed on whether these terms make it harder to convert contractors into permanent hires, especially in SPED roles?

Object 7 ceiling control. In FY 2024–25, Object 7 actual spending exceeded its budgeted amount by more than $10 million. What is the Board’s current expectation for mid-year reporting when Purchased Services is trending over budget?

SB 5263. With the state’s removal of the special education funding cap and increased SPED funding, what information has the Board requested from administration about a plan to build internal capacity and reduce dependency on staffing vendors?

Transition. With a new superintendent scheduled to take over later this year, what information or controls do you believe should be formalized now so the Board is not dependent on informal briefings for visibility into contracted staffing, training, and retention?

Thank you for any corrections or context you can provide.
