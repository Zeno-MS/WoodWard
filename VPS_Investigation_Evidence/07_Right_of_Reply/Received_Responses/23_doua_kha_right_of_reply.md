Thank you for reaching out with your question regarding restraint and isolation oversight in Washington.

 

There is not a standalone oversight mechanism specific solely to restraint and isolation practices at the state level. However, monitoring structures do exist for students with disabilities who are receiving
 special education services.

 

Restraint and isolation data are among the indicators the Special Education Division reviews when selecting districts for special education monitoring activities. These data may inform targeted technical assistance,
 corrective actions, or other monitoring processes when concerns are identified. The use of prohibited restraints may also be addressed through a

special education community complaint process.

 

In addition, the team that monitors nonpublic agencies reviews restraint and isolation data, including the use of prohibited practices. Scott Raub would be the best point of contact for more detailed information
 about those monitoring processes, as I am not directly involved in that specific oversight work.

 

I hope this information is helpful. Please let me know if you would like assistance connecting with the appropriate team.
