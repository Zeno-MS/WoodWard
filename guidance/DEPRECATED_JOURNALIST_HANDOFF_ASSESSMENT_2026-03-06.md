# Journalist Handoff Assessment (March 6, 2026)

## Bottom Line
This is a serious package, and the core reporting is stronger than most self-assembled evidence dumps.

My direct view: it is close, but not fully journalist-ready yet. I would call it roughly **80% to 85% ready**. A competent reporter can immediately see the story, but they will still need to do balancing work, source cleanup, and a few verification passes before publication.

## What Is Strong
- The narrative spine in `06_Articles/` is strong, especially Article 1 and Article 2.
- The framing docs in `EXECUTIVE_SUMMARY.md` and `INVESTIGATION_BACKGROUND_BRIEFING.md` are clear and useful.
- The technical companion in `DATA_DICTIONARY_AND_METHODOLOGY.md` is a real asset.
- The structure is coherent enough that an external journalist can orient quickly.

## What Should Be Added Before Handoff
- A **claim-to-source exhibit index**. One table mapping each major claim to exact source file and page/table.
- A **district-response status memo**. Who replied, who declined, who did not respond, and deadlines provided.
- The missing **VPS 2023-24 F-195/F-195F source file(s)** in `05_Source_Documents/` to support used calculations.
- Full **Amergis contract history** (agreements, amendments, addenda, rate sheets).
- A **rate-escalation timeline** showing actual rate changes over time.
- More **recent (2023-2025) student-impact evidence** tied to staffing instability.
- The cited **union grievance/supporting docs** (text, terms, and any settlement artifacts).
- A short **media-landscape memo** summarizing prior reporting and what this package adds.
- A plain-language **glossary** for Object 7 / F-195 / S-275 / SEBB / DRS / warrant register.
- A compact **visual packet** (3-4 charts) for fast newsroom intake.

## Editorial Caution
The package language sometimes overstates completeness (for example, words like "fully verified" and "finalized") relative to current rebuttal coverage and support-file completeness. The reporting is strong; the packaging should be a little less absolute until remaining gaps are closed.

## How To Expand The Story
- Build the **decision trail**: who prepared estimates, who approved terms, what alternatives were considered.
- Tighten the **student-harm timeline** with contemporary cases from peak-spend years.
- Prove or disprove **internal-capacity failure** with vacancy-duration and recruitment/retention data.
- Strengthen the **outlier case** with robust cross-district actuals, not just budgets.
- Follow the money in contract operations: rate changes, role mix, placement-fee usage, amendment timing.
- Follow oversight interactions to determine whether warnings were raised and ignored.

## High-Value Data To Collect Next
- All Amergis/Maxim contracts, amendments, rate sheets, invoices, and work orders.
- Board packet backup and internal memos for each staffing-vendor approval.
- HR/Special Services/Finance email records on vacancies, fills, and pricing decisions.
- Vacancy and recruiting metrics by role and month.
- Agency staffing roster usage by role and school.
- Missed IEP-minute data, overdue evaluations, complaint logs, and corrective actions.
- Restraint/isolation data by school and year, especially post-2021 ban.
- Union grievances, overload-payment records, bargaining artifacts, and testimony.
- Peer district records (Evergreen, Battle Ground, Camas) for Object 7 actuals and vendor dependence.
- OSPI apportionment advance applications and related correspondence.
- County treasurer and SAO correspondence related to cash stress and controls.
- Corporate continuity records for Amergis/Maxim.

## Readiness Call
This can be handed to journalists now as a **strong investigative starter package**, but it is **not yet an airtight publication package**. Closing the rebuttal, source-completeness, and recent-impact gaps will significantly improve publishability and reduce reporter friction.
