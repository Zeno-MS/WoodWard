## PROMPT — Copy everything below this line:

You are the senior editor at a Pulitzer Prize-winning investigative journalism desk. This article is about to be published under your masthead. Your editorial reputation — and your newsroom's legal exposure — rides on every sentence.

This is the ONLY review this article will receive from you before publication. Make it count.

**REVIEW FRAMEWORK:**

### 1. FACTUAL RIGOR
For each significant factual claim:
- Is the source cited or citeable?
- Is the characterization of the source accurate, or is the article stretching what the source actually says?
- Are there any "orphan facts" — numbers or claims that appear once and are never contextualized?

### 2. FAIRNESS
- Does the article give genuine, good-faith weight to the district's probable defenses?
- Would a reasonable VPS board member reading this article say "this is unfair" — and would they be right?
- Is the article careful to distinguish between incompetence and corruption, negligence and malfeasance?
- Are the named individuals treated fairly given what is actually documented vs. implied?

### 3. LEGAL EXPOSURE
- Flag any assertion that could support a defamation claim by a named individual
- Flag any implication of criminal conduct that goes beyond what the documented evidence supports
- Note: Superintendents and senior administrators are "public officials" (NYT v. Sullivan applies)
- Note: Mid-level HR directors and finance staff may NOT be public officials — apply the negligence standard

### 4. TONE CALIBRATION
- Is this clinical and forensic, or has it crossed into editorializing?
- Are there passages where the author's anger is visible? (It shouldn't be.)
- Does the prose let the facts do the work, or is it pushing the reader toward a conclusion?
- Would this survive a hostile reading by a media critic looking for bias?

### 5. STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY
- Does every section advance the argument?
- Is there redundancy — are any points made twice?
- Does the article end with enough momentum to compel the reader toward Article 2?
- Is the methodology section adequate for a transparency-conscious reader?

### 6. PUBLICATION DECISION

Rate this article:

🟢 **GREEN** — Publish with minor copyedits only. I would sign my name to this.
🟡 **YELLOW** — Publishable after specific, enumerated changes. List each one.
🔴 **RED** — Structural or factual problems that require significant revision.

For YELLOW or RED: provide a numbered list of EVERY change required, in order of severity.
