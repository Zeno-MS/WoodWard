# WHERE THE MONEY WENT

## An Investigation into Vancouver Public Schools' Escalating Contractor Spending and the Vendor With a $150 Million Fraud Settlement on Its Record

*By Project WoodWard — Draft 1*

---

In a single fiscal year, Vancouver Public Schools wired **$27,904,823** to a healthcare staffing company called Amergis. Not over a decade. Not across a bond measure. In twelve months.

That sum — enough to hire more than 350 full-time classroom teachers at district salary — flowed quietly through the district's Warrant Registers, a set of public financial documents that the Board of Directors approves, typically in bulk, at the end of each monthly meeting. No press release announced it. No competitive bid document is publicly visible on the district's BoardDocs portal. The spending simply appeared, line by line, invoice by invoice, across 193 scanned PDF files obtained and analyzed by this investigation.

---

## The Nut Graph

This investigation has found that Vancouver Public Schools dramatically accelerated its payments to Amergis Healthcare Staffing — a company that is, by all forensic indicators, the re-named successor to **Maxim Healthcare Services**, which settled for **$150 million** in 2011 to resolve federal criminal and civil charges of Medicaid fraud, including billing for services never performed and paying kickbacks for client referrals (U.S. Department of Justice, October 2011).

The transition was not disclosed to the public. In the 2022-23 school year, VPS paid Maxim $14,439,314 and Amergis $0. By 2024-25, it paid Maxim $46,625 — a rounding error — and Amergis **$27,904,823**. The rebrand was complete. The invoices kept coming.

---

## The Rate Sheet No One Talked About

Buried in a 2021 contract renewal attachment — obtained from the district's own BoardDocs system — is a document titled **"Attachment A: Vancouver Public Schools STAFFING RATES."** It is signed by Maxim Healthcare and lists the billing rates the district agreed to pay, effective May 5, 2021:

| Role | Rate (Per Hour) |
|------|----------------|
| Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA) | $85.00–$115.00 |
| School Psychologist | $90.00–$110.00 |
| Speech-Language Pathologist (SLP) | $80.00–$95.00 |
| Registered Nurse (RN) | $70.00 |
| Paraprofessional | $37.00–$45.00 |

These rates are not inherently unreasonable for specialized healthcare staffing in isolation. The problem is what came next.

**Section 5.5 — Annual Rate Increases**: *"EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTION agrees to and accepts annual rate increases at the percentage listed on Attachment A of this Agreement."*

**Section 5.4 — Rate Change**: *"MAXIM will provide EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTION at least thirty (30) days advance written notice of any change in rates."*

Read those two provisions together: the district signed an agreement surrendering its right to negotiate rates year-over-year, requiring only 30 days notice from the vendor to change billing schedules, with no stated ceiling. VPS locked itself into a vendor-controlled ratchet mechanism on a multi-million dollar relationship.

That was 2021. By 2024-25, that relationship cost taxpayers **$27.9 million** — a 5x increase from the prior year.

## The Rebrand the Board Never Mentioned

Maxim Healthcare Services rebranded to **Amergis Healthcare Staffing** in 2022 — the same year Superintendent Jeff Snell received a **$71,315 salary increase**, bringing his compensation to $363,684, a **24% single-year jump** (OSPI S-275 Personnel Database, 2022-23).

The district's payments shifted from Maxim to Amergis beginning in fiscal year 2023-24, when both vendor names appear in the Warrant Registers simultaneously: $8.1 million to Maxim, $5.4 million to Amergis. That overlap is the fingerprint of a controlled transition. By 2024-25, the handoff was complete.

VPS board minutes reviewed for this investigation contain no public discussion of the vendor name change, no disclosure that the district's primary staffing provider had rebranded following the largest Medicaid fraud settlement in Washington State healthcare contractor history, and no competitive rebid of the contract.

## The Staffing Paradox

The surge in contract spending is not explained by a parallel cut in district staff. Vancouver's 2023-24 staffing benchmarks show the district maintains a heavier administrative structure than comparable districts — a **1:33 administrator-to-teacher ratio**, compared to neighboring Evergreen School District's 1:37.

It raises a question the Warrant Registers alone cannot answer: who is being replaced by these contractor invoices?

The district's reported paraprofessional FTE — traditionally the workforce that provides one-on-one special education support — is **28.27 full-time equivalents**. For a district of Vancouver's size serving students with legally mandated Individual Education Plans (IEPs), that number is anomalously low. It suggests the in-house workforce for these federally mandated services has been systematically replaced by contractor billing — at rates the district's own contract says it cannot negotiate down.

## Missing Links — What This Investigation Still Needs

1. **The Amergis Rate Sheet**: Does the 2022-2024 Amergis contract replicate Maxim's uncapped annual increase provision? This is the most important unanswered question.
2. **The Board Authorization**: Which board meeting, which agenda item, and which vote authorized the Amergis contract? Was it buried in a consent agenda?
3. **The Revolving Door**: Have any former VPS administrators, HR staff, or procurement officers moved to employment at Amergis, Soliant, or ProCare Therapy?
4. **The Peer Comparison**: VPS paid $27.9M to Amergis alone in 2024-25. What did Evergreen, Battle Ground, and Spokane pay for comparable services in the same year?
