# The Cabinet Restructuring & "Shell Game" Analysis

Here is the data regarding the district's claim that they "repurposed" the Deputy Superintendent role to save money:

**The Knowledge Graph Limitation (The Mask):**
The specific local titles "Deputy Superintendent" and "Executive Director of Teaching and Learning" are **not** discretely mapped as nodes in the graph because the OSPI S-275 data standardizes them under generic Duty Roots (e.g., Duty Root 12 for Deputy/Assistant Supt, Duty Root 13 for "Other District Administrator", and 21 for "Director/Supervisor"). The district hides its administrative bloat behind these generic codes.

**The Financial Reality (The Salary Bands):**
By cross-referencing the top 40 earners from 2023-2024 to 2024-2025 and our historical forensic research, we can extract the true salary bands:
*   **Archived "Deputy Superintendent" Band:** ~$240,000
*   **New "Executive Director" Band (Duty Roots 13 & 21):** Extracted from 2024-25 top earners (e.g., William Oman, James Gray, Janell Ephraim, Jeff Fish): **$210,000 - $220,000**

**Conclusion on "Payroll Savings":**
The elimination of the Deputy Superintendent role did not generate the $240,000 in savings implied by "eliminating a position." Because the headcount was retained and simply shifted laterally into expanded "Executive Director" silos (which command ~$218,000+ per year), the actual payroll savings at the cabinet level is **marginal (roughly $20,000 - $30,000 per repurposed FTE)**. The district retained the bulk of the salary cost, the pension liabilities, and the office space. This mathematically supports Larry Roe's critique that the district is playing a "Shell Game" with titles to obscure the lack of actual central office cuts.
