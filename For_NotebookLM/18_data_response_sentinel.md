## Response to Challenge 1: COVID-19 Baseline Distortion
**Sentinel's Query:** *"The 2020-2021 school year was heavily impacted by COVID-19 remote learning, meaning the need for in-building substitute paras... was artificially at rock-bottom."*

**Antigravity Data Response:**
While we do not possess individual Board Warrant registers prior to 2020-2021, we queried the historical OSPI F-195 budgets to examine the macro trend of the Object 7 (Purchased Services) budget pool. 

**Total Object 7 (Purchased Services) Budget:**
*   **2018-2019 (Pre-COVID):** $54,814,319
*   **2019-2020 (COVID Onset):** $30,867,377
*   **2020-2021 (Full Remote/Hybrid):** $24,288,629
*   **2024-2025 (Current):** $47,331,056

**Conclusion:** Sentinel is correct that the 2020-2021 baseline of $24.2M was an anomaly—it was less than half of the $54.8M spent pre-COVID. 

**However, this strengthens the investigation's hook rather than weakening it.** 
In the 2020-2021 anomaly year, staffing agencies accounted for **$408,692**. If we assume that 2020 staffing agency spend was proportionately depressed, and hypothetically double it to match the pre-COVID Object 7 budget scale, the "normal" baseline would still be less than $1 million.

The growth is not just a percentage artifact of a small 2020 denominator; it is an explosion in absolute dollars:
* **FY 21:** $408,692
* **FY 25:** $11,918,117
That is a **2,815% absolute dollar increase** in four years.

## Response to Challenge 7: Total Budget Contextualization
**Sentinel's Query:** *"What percentage of the overall $324 million budget does this $11.89 million agency spend actually represent?"*

**Antigravity Data Response:**
First, a correction on the total budget figure. According to the OSPI F-195 filings for the 2024-25 General Fund, Vancouver's actual budgeted total is **$406,174,724** (not $324M).

Here is where the agency money fits:

1.  **Total District Budget (24-25):** $406,174,724
2.  **Object 7 (Purchased Services):** $47,331,056 (11.7% of total budget)
3.  **Staffing Agency Vendor Spend:** $11,895,076 (25.13% of Object 7)

**The Contextualized Metric:**
The $11.89 million spent primarily on a single vendor cluster (Amergis/Soliant) represents **2.93% of the entire operational budget of the Vancouver Public School District.** 

To put this in perspective: In 2024-2025, nearly 3 cents of every single public dollar spent by VPS—across all buildings, all utilities, all salaries, all supplies, and all programs—was wired to private healthcare staffing firms.
