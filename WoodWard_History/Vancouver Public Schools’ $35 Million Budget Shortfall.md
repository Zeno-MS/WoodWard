# Vancouver Public Schools' \$35 Million Budget Shortfall

## Officially Stated Causes of the Deficit

Vancouver Public Schools (VPS) entered the 2024--25 school year with an approximately **\$35 million budget shortfall**, requiring drastic cuts of about 8--10% of its budget[\[1\]](https://vansd.org/we-need-your-input-budget-reductions-2024-25/#:~:text=Our%202024,F%C3%B3%C3%B3sun%20Chuuk)[\[2\]](https://katu.com/news/local/vancouver-public-schools-facing-financial-uncertainty-considers-35m-in-staff-cuts#:~:text=A%20resolution%20that%20the%20board,a%20decrease%20in%20state%20support). District officials and board documents cite several converging causes for this financial crisis:

- **Enrollment Declines:** Like many districts post-pandemic, VPS has seen a drop in student enrollment, which directly reduces state funding (Washington funds schools on a per-pupil basis)[\[2\]](https://katu.com/news/local/vancouver-public-schools-facing-financial-uncertainty-considers-35m-in-staff-cuts#:~:text=A%20resolution%20that%20the%20board,a%20decrease%20in%20state%20support)[\[3\]](https://katu.com/news/investigations/facing-multi-million-dollar-cuts-washington-school-districts-point-state-funding-fix-money-students-teachers-politics-budget-camas-battle-ground-clark-county-vancouver#:~:text=Image%3A%20Anzalone%20said%20Camas%20lost,student%20basis.%20%28KATU). Enrollment has not rebounded to pre-2020 levels, and fewer incoming kindergarteners are replacing graduating seniors, creating a structural funding decline[\[3\]](https://katu.com/news/investigations/facing-multi-million-dollar-cuts-washington-school-districts-point-state-funding-fix-money-students-teachers-politics-budget-camas-battle-ground-clark-county-vancouver#:~:text=Image%3A%20Anzalone%20said%20Camas%20lost,student%20basis.%20%28KATU)[\[4\]](https://katu.com/news/investigations/facing-multi-million-dollar-cuts-washington-school-districts-point-state-funding-fix-money-students-teachers-politics-budget-camas-battle-ground-clark-county-vancouver#:~:text=Schools%20are%20also%20dealing%20with,keeping%20up%20with%20graduating%20classes). (State data shows a rise in homeschooling and private school enrollment in Clark County since 2019, contributing to public school enrollment losses[\[5\]](https://katu.com/news/investigations/facing-multi-million-dollar-cuts-washington-school-districts-point-state-funding-fix-money-students-teachers-politics-budget-camas-battle-ground-clark-county-vancouver#:~:text=classes%20aren%E2%80%99t%20keeping%20up%20with,graduating%20classes).)

- **Expiration of Federal COVID Relief (ESSER) Funds:** VPS benefited from one-time federal pandemic aid (ESSER funds) that helped plug budget holes in 2020--2022. With those **COVID-19 relief funds now dried up**, the district is losing a key revenue source that had been used to bridge gaps[\[2\]](https://katu.com/news/local/vancouver-public-schools-facing-financial-uncertainty-considers-35m-in-staff-cuts#:~:text=A%20resolution%20that%20the%20board,a%20decrease%20in%20state%20support)[\[6\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=Facing%20multi,how%20the%20state%20funds%20schools). This **"ESSER cliff"** left VPS without millions in supplemental funding for staffing and programs. *District leaders acknowledge that some of this one-time money was used to hire or retain staff to support student needs, which was not sustainable long-term[\[7\]](https://katu.com/news/investigations/facing-multi-million-dollar-cuts-washington-school-districts-point-state-funding-fix-money-students-teachers-politics-budget-camas-battle-ground-clark-county-vancouver#:~:text=Many%20districts%20have%20blamed%20expiring,time%20money%20to%20hire%20people).*

- **Inadequate State Funding Formula:** Washington's **school funding model** (the prototypical school formula) does not fully cover the actual staffing and program costs at VPS. The district and observers note a persistent **gap between what the state funds and what it costs to run the schools**[\[8\]](https://www.kptv.com/2024/03/14/vancouver-public-schools-approve-staff-cuts-meet-35-million-shortfall/#:~:text=As%20to%20how%20the%20district,is%20simply%20not%20enough%20funding)[\[9\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=The%20problem%20with%20that%20funding,it%20receives%20from%20the%20state). For example, VPS employs more staff (and at higher wages) than the state's formula allocates; one analysis found VPS spends about **\$22.8 million more on teacher salaries and benefits than it receives from the state** due to this misalignment[\[9\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=The%20problem%20with%20that%20funding,it%20receives%20from%20the%20state). District officials have openly called the state funding model "misaligned with the real-world costs" of operating a school system[\[10\]](https://vansd.org/2024-25-budget-information/#:~:text=Our%20VPS%20school%20board%20recently,of%20running%20a%20school%20district).

- **Loss of Local Reserves and Stopgap Funds:** In recent years, VPS relied on temporary funding sources -- like federal aid and its **reserve funds** -- to cover budget shortfalls. Those **bridging revenue sources have gone away**, leaving no cushion[\[11\]](https://vansd.org/2024-25-budget-information/#:~:text=Why%20did%20we%20need%20to,understanding%20the%20budget). By 2023--24, VPS had effectively **depleted its operational reserves**, creating a cash-flow crunch[\[12\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/opinion/letter-vps-has-no-operational-reserves-and-no-credible-plans-to-raise-them/#:~:text=VPS%20has%20no%20operational%20reserves,part%20of%20school%20district%20budgeting). The district even requested emergency advances on state apportionments (essentially short-term loans) to meet payroll -- borrowing **\$15 million in January 2024 and another \$18 million in October 2024**[\[12\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/opinion/letter-vps-has-no-operational-reserves-and-no-credible-plans-to-raise-them/#:~:text=VPS%20has%20no%20operational%20reserves,part%20of%20school%20district%20budgeting).

- **Rising Costs (Inflation and Compensation):** The cost of running schools has climbed due to inflation in supplies, services, and utilities[\[13\]](https://katu.com/news/local/vancouver-public-schools-facing-financial-uncertainty-considers-35m-in-staff-cuts#:~:text=The%20cuts%20will%20account%20for,increase%20in%20costs%20from%20inflation). More significantly, **personnel costs** (which are over 85% of the budget) have risen from salary increases and higher benefit costs[\[14\]](https://vansd.org/2024-25-budget-information/#:~:text=school%20district)[\[15\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/news/forensic-accountant-attempts-to-unravel-impending-school-budget-crisis/#:~:text=Couch%20has%20spent%20the%20time,%E2%80%9D). VPS Superintendent Jeff Snell noted that **staffing costs have outpaced funding**, creating a structural gap[\[8\]](https://www.kptv.com/2024/03/14/vancouver-public-schools-approve-staff-cuts-meet-35-million-shortfall/#:~:text=As%20to%20how%20the%20district,is%20simply%20not%20enough%20funding). Much of this is tied to **salary raises** negotiated in recent years -- for instance, after a 2018 statewide school funding overhaul, many teachers received substantial pay increases, locking in higher ongoing expenses[\[15\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/news/forensic-accountant-attempts-to-unravel-impending-school-budget-crisis/#:~:text=Couch%20has%20spent%20the%20time,%E2%80%9D). These increased obligations, coupled with inflation, have further strained the budget.

In official statements, the VPS board summarized that the district faces "**financial uncertainty, an enrollment decline, decreased federal funding, and a decrease in state support**"[\[2\]](https://katu.com/news/local/vancouver-public-schools-facing-financial-uncertainty-considers-35m-in-staff-cuts#:~:text=A%20resolution%20that%20the%20board,a%20decrease%20in%20state%20support). In short, **fewer students**, **vanishing COVID funds**, an **insufficient state funding formula**, and **higher operating costs** all combined to push Vancouver Public Schools into a \$35 million hole.

## Critical Perspectives and Unspoken Factors

Beyond the district's own explanations, local educators, union leaders, and community members have offered critical perspectives on how VPS reached this crisis. These viewpoints highlight **unsaid factors** or question the district's decisions:

- **Spending Beyond Means:** Some critics argue that VPS leadership made budgeting choices that assumed revenue the district didn't actually have. Vancouver resident Larry Roe, in a letter referencing an October 2025 board meeting, pointed out that for **three consecutive years the district spent more than it received -- overspending by \$9 million, \$15 million, and \$7 million** each year[\[16\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/opinion/letter-vps-has-no-operational-reserves-and-no-credible-plans-to-raise-them/#:~:text=Our%20Vancouver%20Public%20School%20Board,for%20the%20current%20school%20year). This structural deficit spending eroded the district's reserves. Roe notes VPS "has no operational reserves and no credible plans to raise them," essentially living paycheck-to-paycheck and then relying on bailouts[\[12\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/opinion/letter-vps-has-no-operational-reserves-and-no-credible-plans-to-raise-them/#:~:text=VPS%20has%20no%20operational%20reserves,part%20of%20school%20district%20budgeting). This critique implies that **VPS failed to rein in expenses** (or seek new funding) as deficits accumulated, leading to the cash crunch.

- **Reliance on One-Time Funds for Ongoing Costs:** A related criticism is the use of **one-time money to fund recurring expenses** -- essentially kicking the can down the road. **Federal ESSER funds** and local levy dollars intended for "enrichment" were used to preserve programs and staffing that the state wouldn't fully fund[\[17\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/opinion/letter-vps-has-no-operational-reserves-and-no-credible-plans-to-raise-them/#:~:text=In%20practice%2C%20we%20used%20money,the%20gap%3A%20our%20local%20levies)[\[7\]](https://katu.com/news/investigations/facing-multi-million-dollar-cuts-washington-school-districts-point-state-funding-fix-money-students-teachers-politics-budget-camas-battle-ground-clark-county-vancouver#:~:text=Many%20districts%20have%20blamed%20expiring,time%20money%20to%20hire%20people). Camas Superintendent John Anzalone (from a neighboring district) admitted districts likely **"held on to... incredible \[staff\] for maybe a little bit too long" with the federal money**[\[7\]](https://katu.com/news/investigations/facing-multi-million-dollar-cuts-washington-school-districts-point-state-funding-fix-money-students-teachers-politics-budget-camas-battle-ground-clark-county-vancouver#:~:text=Many%20districts%20have%20blamed%20expiring,time%20money%20to%20hire%20people). When those funds expired, districts like VPS were left with commitments (staff positions, programs) that could no longer be paid for -- a factor officials were perhaps reluctant to emphasize while the money lasted.

- **Impact of 2018 Salary Increases:** Some analysis traces the roots of the problem back to the **2018 McCleary funding settlement** in Washington. That statewide reform injected new state funds for schools but capped local levies; it also led to significant **teacher pay raises** after union strikes. Forensic accountant Tiffany Couch, who studied local districts' finances, warned in 2019 that **"these school districts can't afford the raises they gave"** in the wake of the McCleary deal[\[15\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/news/forensic-accountant-attempts-to-unravel-impending-school-budget-crisis/#:~:text=Couch%20has%20spent%20the%20time,%E2%80%9D). In VPS's case, those raises immediately contributed to an expected deficit (over \$11 million for 2019--20) and necessitated cuts even then[\[18\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/news/forensic-accountant-attempts-to-unravel-impending-school-budget-crisis/#:~:text=Much%20of%20her%20hour,500%2C000%20hit%20against%20their%20budget). Critics suggest this early warning was an "unsaid" factor -- that **VPS's salary and staffing levels were set above what its long-term revenues could support**, a gap temporarily masked by short-term funds.

- **Questionable Budget Priorities:** Union representatives have generally acknowledged the funding bind but have **disagreed with some cuts** and priorities chosen by administrators[\[19\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=Union%20leaders%20from%20both%20districts,number%20on%20the%20eastern%20half)[\[20\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=Little%20room%20for%20input). For example, the Vancouver Education Association noted disappointment that after over an hour of public testimony pleading for alternatives, the board **approved the \$35 million cut package without modifications or deeper discussion** of community suggestions[\[21\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=Although%20Anderson%20understands%20the%20difficult,discussing%20if%20it%20should%20change). In Evergreen (the neighboring district), union members criticized the decision to eliminate all teacher-librarian positions as harmful to students' literacy, implying that different budget choices could have been made[\[22\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=In%20Evergreen%2C%20teachers%20union%20member,students%20who%20have%20trouble%20reading). In VPS, union leaders have called for more involvement in deciding cuts and more transparency on spending. While VPS did cut central office roles and even reduced the superintendent's salary by 15% as part of the cuts[\[23\]](https://katu.com/news/local/vancouver-public-schools-facing-financial-uncertainty-considers-35m-in-staff-cuts#:~:text=The%20cuts%20include%20more%20than,counselors%2C%20and%209%20teacher%20librarians)[\[24\]](https://katu.com/news/local/vancouver-public-schools-facing-financial-uncertainty-considers-35m-in-staff-cuts#:~:text=It%20also%20eliminates%2019%20positions,2), some in the community still question whether **administrative costs and non-classroom expenses were trimmed enough** before impacting classrooms.

- **State Funding and Mandates:** Both district officials and critical observers point to the state's role. VPS's board and letter-writers alike highlight that **many spending requirements (e.g. special education, staffing ratios) are set by state or federal law, but funding for them is insufficient**, forcing districts to cover the difference[\[25\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/opinion/letter-vps-has-no-operational-reserves-and-no-credible-plans-to-raise-them/#:~:text=Our%20current%20VPS%20School%20Board,all%20participate%20in%20these%20appeals)[\[6\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=Facing%20multi,how%20the%20state%20funds%20schools). This means VPS was essentially **mandated to spend beyond what the state provides**, a systemic issue beyond local control. The Washington Education Association (teachers' union) echoed that it's unacceptable for districts to have to **borrow money to fund basic operations** and pleaded for the state to fully fund K--12 education[\[26\]](https://www.facebook.com/WEAAdvocacy/photos/our-kids-deserve-better-school-districts-should-not-have-to-borrow-money-to-supp/1303671668469434/#:~:text=WEA%20%7C%20Facebook%20,teaching%20and%20supports%20that). The **finger-pointing between state vs. district responsibility** is a subtext: critics say VPS overspent, but VPS leaders respond that they were simply trying to meet student needs that the state underfunded[\[25\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/opinion/letter-vps-has-no-operational-reserves-and-no-credible-plans-to-raise-them/#:~:text=Our%20current%20VPS%20School%20Board,all%20participate%20in%20these%20appeals)[\[27\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=At%20the%20same%20time%2C%20enrollment,receiving%20federal%20COVID%20relief%20funding).

In summary, critical perspectives acknowledge the well-known factors (enrollment decline, funding shortfall) but also shine light on **VPS's budgeting practices**. Years of operating above revenue, **using reserves and temporary funds to avoid painful cuts**, set the stage for this crisis. The tension is evident: VPS leaders argue their hand was forced by inadequate funding, while skeptics believe **better planning or different choices** could have softened the blow.

## Timeline of Key Budget Decisions Leading to the Crisis

To understand how VPS arrived at a \$35 million deficit, it's useful to look at the timeline of budget decisions and turning points over recent years:

- **2018:** Washington's response to the *McCleary* court ruling boosted state education funding but restricted local levies. That summer, teachers across the state (including Clark County) went on strike seeking large pay raises. VPS granted substantial **salary increases** that fall. *Analysis soon warned that these raises were not fiscally sustainable without new revenue*[\[15\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/news/forensic-accountant-attempts-to-unravel-impending-school-budget-crisis/#:~:text=Couch%20has%20spent%20the%20time,%E2%80%9D).

- **2019:** By early 2019, VPS projected an **\$11.4 million deficit for the 2019--20 school year**[\[18\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/news/forensic-accountant-attempts-to-unravel-impending-school-budget-crisis/#:~:text=Much%20of%20her%20hour,500%2C000%20hit%20against%20their%20budget). The district announced plans to trim expenses and possibly staff to balance the budget[\[18\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/news/forensic-accountant-attempts-to-unravel-impending-school-budget-crisis/#:~:text=Much%20of%20her%20hour,500%2C000%20hit%20against%20their%20budget). (A new union contract in 2019 added further costs, slightly worsening the gap[\[28\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/news/forensic-accountant-attempts-to-unravel-impending-school-budget-crisis/#:~:text=2019,500%2C000%20hit%20against%20their%20budget).) **Initial cuts** and hiring freezes were implemented around this time to address the shortfall.

- **2020--2021 (Pandemic Years):** The COVID-19 pandemic led to an abrupt enrollment drop as some families left public schools. However, VPS received several rounds of **federal emergency aid (ESSER I/II/III)** which it used from 2020 through 2022 to maintain programs and even invest in technology and added student support staff. These funds acted as a **temporary patch** in budgets that otherwise might have been in deficit[\[29\]](https://katu.com/news/investigations/facing-multi-million-dollar-cuts-washington-school-districts-point-state-funding-fix-money-students-teachers-politics-budget-camas-battle-ground-clark-county-vancouver#:~:text=KATU%20learned%20the%20budget%20woes,government%20that%20patched%20the%20problem)[\[7\]](https://katu.com/news/investigations/facing-multi-million-dollar-cuts-washington-school-districts-point-state-funding-fix-money-students-teachers-politics-budget-camas-battle-ground-clark-county-vancouver#:~:text=Many%20districts%20have%20blamed%20expiring,time%20money%20to%20hire%20people). During these years, major cuts were largely postponed, and the district tapped its **fund balance ("rainy day" reserves)** when needed to cover any overages.

- **2022--2023:** Enrollment recovery was sluggish -- VPS's student population remained **significantly below pre-pandemic levels**[\[27\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=At%20the%20same%20time%2C%20enrollment,receiving%20federal%20COVID%20relief%20funding). Meanwhile, costs continued to rise (employee COLA raises, inflation in utilities and supplies). By late 2022, VPS's reserve funds were nearly exhausted due to successive years of deficit spending[\[16\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/opinion/letter-vps-has-no-operational-reserves-and-no-credible-plans-to-raise-them/#:~:text=Our%20Vancouver%20Public%20School%20Board,for%20the%20current%20school%20year). District leadership began warning of a looming "fiscal cliff" when ESSER funds would run out in 2024. In **November 2023**, a budget update to the school board outlined the anticipated need to cut roughly 8--10% from the coming year's budget given the revenue shortfall[\[1\]](https://vansd.org/we-need-your-input-budget-reductions-2024-25/#:~:text=Our%202024,F%C3%B3%C3%B3sun%20Chuuk).

- **Early 2024:** The budget crisis came to a head. In **January 2024**, VPS had to request an **emergency apportionment advance** from the state (about **\$15 million** in early delivery of funds) just to maintain cash flow[\[12\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/opinion/letter-vps-has-no-operational-reserves-and-no-credible-plans-to-raise-them/#:~:text=VPS%20has%20no%20operational%20reserves,part%20of%20school%20district%20budgeting). The superintendent launched a community budget survey in February 2024, seeking input on where to cut 8--10% of expenditures[\[1\]](https://vansd.org/we-need-your-input-budget-reductions-2024-25/#:~:text=Our%202024,F%C3%B3%C3%B3sun%20Chuuk)[\[30\]](https://vansd.org/we-need-your-input-budget-reductions-2024-25/#:~:text=More%20about%20our%20budget%20challenges,2025%20budget). By **March 2024**, a detailed reduction plan was proposed: roughly **262 full-time positions** would be eliminated, including over 130 teachers, dozens of support staff, and even some administrators[\[31\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=Anticipating%20a%20%2435%20million%20budget,Association%20of%20Educational%20Support%20Professionals)[\[23\]](https://katu.com/news/local/vancouver-public-schools-facing-financial-uncertainty-considers-35m-in-staff-cuts#:~:text=The%20cuts%20include%20more%20than,counselors%2C%20and%209%20teacher%20librarians). On **March 12, 2024, the VPS Board of Directors unanimously approved \$35 million in budget reductions** for the 2024--25 school year[\[32\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=and%20Evergreen%20Public%20Schools%20serves,number%20on%20the%20eastern%20half). Board members voiced regret, calling it a "heartbreaking" decision but one made unavoidable by the fiscal realities[\[33\]](https://www.kptv.com/2024/03/14/vancouver-public-schools-approve-staff-cuts-meet-35-million-shortfall/#:~:text=School%20board%20members%20were%20emotional,delivering%20the%20tough%20vote)[\[34\]](https://www.kptv.com/2024/03/14/vancouver-public-schools-approve-staff-cuts-meet-35-million-shortfall/#:~:text=costs%20and%20said%20there%20is,simply%20not%20enough%20funding).

- **Mid/Late 2024:** The approved cuts took effect as the 2024--25 school year began -- class sizes rose modestly due to fewer teachers, and some programs were scaled back. In **October 2024**, VPS again requested an early draw on state funds (\~**\$18 million**) to cover expenses, indicating continued cash flow stress[\[12\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/opinion/letter-vps-has-no-operational-reserves-and-no-credible-plans-to-raise-them/#:~:text=VPS%20has%20no%20operational%20reserves,part%20of%20school%20district%20budgeting). The district also explored external help; for example, in fall 2024 it requested **\$21.4 million in special state emergency funding**, of which only \$8.7 million was granted[\[35\]](https://www.columbian.com/news/2025/nov/21/vancouver-public-schools-sought-21-4-million-from-state-to-bridge-cash-flow-shortages-it-got-8-7-million/#:~:text=Vancouver%20Public%20Schools%20sought%20%2421,35M%20from%20budget%20in). These measures underscored the ongoing budget imbalance even after the cuts.

- **2025 and Beyond:** Despite the 2024 cuts, projections showed multi-million deficits continuing in future years (e.g. an **estimated \$20 million shortfall in 2026--27** if nothing changes)[\[36\]](https://www.columbian.com/news/2025/dec/10/vancouver-school-board-approves-borrowing-from-clark-county-treasurers-office-amid-funding-shortfall/#:~:text=Vancouver%20school%20board%20approves%20borrowing,changes%20to%20alternative%20learning). In August 2025, the school board even approved borrowing from the Clark County Treasurer's Office to ensure the district could pay its bills -- an unprecedented step born of its empty reserves[\[37\]](https://www.columbian.com/news/2025/dec/10/vancouver-school-board-approves-borrowing-from-clark-county-treasurers-office-amid-funding-shortfall/#:~:text=,changes%20to%20alternative%20learning). VPS and other districts ramped up advocacy in 2025, urging the state legislature to increase K--12 funding to prevent further local cuts[\[38\]](https://vansd.org/2024-25-budget-information/#:~:text=You%20can%20help%20us%20by,our%20Vancouver%20Public%20Schools%20students). By the 2025--26 budget cycle, VPS aimed to rebuild a small reserve and avoid mid-year insolvency, but officials warned that without significant **new state support or local revenue**, the district may face additional program cuts or state oversight in the coming years[\[39\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/opinion/letter-vps-has-no-operational-reserves-and-no-credible-plans-to-raise-them/#:~:text=school%20district%20budgeting)[\[40\]](https://www.clarkcountytoday.com/opinion/letter-vps-has-no-operational-reserves-and-no-credible-plans-to-raise-them/#:~:text=If%20the%20State%20does%20not,the%20Superintendent%20of%20Public%20Instruction).

This timeline illustrates that Vancouver's financial crisis was **years in the making** -- a combination of policy changes, economic shocks, and budget decisions spanning late 2010s to mid-2020s. Each step (from 2018 pay raises to using one-time funds through 2023) traded short-term gains for long-term pain, culminating in the drastic measures of 2024.

## Comparison with Evergreen and Battle Ground School Districts

A key question is whether Vancouver Public Schools' situation is unique or part of a broader regional trend. **Comparative analysis with neighboring districts** shows that while VPS's shortfall is larger in absolute terms, many nearby districts are facing **similar challenges** rather than VPS being an outlier:

- **Evergreen Public Schools (EPS):** Evergreen is Vancouver's eastern neighboring district of comparable size (about 23,000 students). In the same 2024--25 budget cycle, **Evergreen faced roughly a \$19--20 million deficit**, about 8% of its budget[\[41\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=Meanwhile%20the%20board%20of%20Evergreen,SEIU%20Local%201948)[\[42\]](https://www.kptv.com/2024/03/14/vancouver-public-schools-approve-staff-cuts-meet-35-million-shortfall/#:~:text=opposite%20direction%20of%20where%20you,said%20a%20high%20school%20student). In March 2024, Evergreen's board approved cutting **140 full-time positions** to close a \$19 million gap[\[41\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=Meanwhile%20the%20board%20of%20Evergreen,SEIU%20Local%201948). This included dozens of teacher layoffs (Evergreen even initially proposed eliminating all librarian positions, sparking a petition by staff)[\[22\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=In%20Evergreen%2C%20teachers%20union%20member,students%20who%20have%20trouble%20reading). Evergreen's causes mirror VPS's: declining enrollment and insufficient state funding post-ESSER. Notably, Evergreen managed to avoid completely depleting reserves, but still had to make painful cuts and warned of more to come if state funding doesn't improve.

- **Battle Ground Public Schools:** Battle Ground, a smaller district north of Vancouver, also encountered significant shortfalls. For 2024--25, Battle Ground cut about **\$8.5 million** in costs and then in 2025--26 faced another **\$14 million in reductions** to stay balanced[\[43\]](https://swweducation.org/battle-ground-school-district-budget-deficits/#:~:text=Battle%20Ground%20School%20District%20%E2%80%93,the%20district%20to%20provide)[\[44\]](https://www.columbian.com/news/2025/aug/26/battle-ground-school-board-oks-2025-26-budget-with-14m-in-cuts/#:~:text=Battle%20Ground%20school%20board%20OKs,with%20%2414%20million%20in%20cuts). A news report noted Battle Ground "faced \$20 million" in budget strain around this period[\[45\]](https://katu.com/news/investigations/facing-multi-million-dollar-cuts-washington-school-districts-point-state-funding-fix-money-students-teachers-politics-budget-camas-battle-ground-clark-county-vancouver#:~:text=%E2%80%9CIt%27s%20unfortunate,about%20it%2C%E2%80%9D%20Ternan%20said). One factor for BG was a drop in local levy revenue due to voter-approved changes, alongside the same enrollment and inflation pressures. By cutting early and often (and with voters approving a supplemental levy for capital needs in 2024), Battle Ground's deficits have been a bit less dire than Vancouver's, but the district still had to use all of its reserve by mid-2024[\[46\]](https://www.battlegroundps.org/article/1919234#:~:text=Schools%20www,used%20all%20of%20its).

- **Other Districts (Region & State):** The pattern extends across Clark County and beyond. **Camas School District** (smaller, affluent suburb) had to cut about **\$15 million (10--12%)** of its budget for 2024--25[\[47\]](https://katu.com/news/investigations/facing-multi-million-dollar-cuts-washington-school-districts-point-state-funding-fix-money-students-teachers-politics-budget-camas-battle-ground-clark-county-vancouver#:~:text=Camas%20Superintendent%20Dr,told%20KATU)[\[48\]](https://katu.com/news/investigations/facing-multi-million-dollar-cuts-washington-school-districts-point-state-funding-fix-money-students-teachers-politics-budget-camas-battle-ground-clark-county-vancouver#:~:text=His%20district%20announced%20cuts%20up,cuts%20will%20reach%20the%20classroom). **Washougal, Hockinson, and Ridgefield** -- even smaller districts in the area -- all reported budget shortfalls requiring staff or program cuts in 2024. This trend is not confined to Washington: across the river in Oregon, major districts like Portland and Salem are also slashing tens of millions due to the end of COVID funds and rising costs[\[49\]](https://www.opb.org/article/2024/03/21/oregon-southwest-washington-school-districts-face-significant-budget-cuts/#:~:text=Inadequate%20funding%20from%20the%20state%2C,strikes%20%20to%20%2012)[\[50\]](https://www.opb.org/article/2024/03/21/oregon-southwest-washington-school-districts-face-significant-budget-cuts/#:~:text=Oregon%E2%80%99s%20largest%20districts%20will%20feel,needing%20to%20cut%20%2460%20million). What sets Vancouver apart is mainly the **scale** of the shortfall (\$35 million is one of the largest single-district gaps in the region), but proportionally it represents a similar \~9% cut that many others are also experiencing[\[1\]](https://vansd.org/we-need-your-input-budget-reductions-2024-25/#:~:text=Our%202024,F%C3%B3%C3%B3sun%20Chuuk)[\[51\]](https://katu.com/news/investigations/facing-multi-million-dollar-cuts-washington-school-districts-point-state-funding-fix-money-students-teachers-politics-budget-camas-battle-ground-clark-county-vancouver#:~:text=Other%20districts%20in%20Clark%20County,are%20facing%20reduction%20as%20well).

In essence, **Vancouver Public Schools is not alone** in its budget woes. The same combination of **enrollment decline, the ESSER funding cliff, and state funding limitations** has hit districts across the county and state. Evergreen's \~\$20 million and Battle Ground's \~\$14 million in cuts underscore that Vancouver's crisis is part of a **systemic funding problem**, not merely an isolated case of mismanagement[\[6\]](https://nwlaborpress.org/2024/04/vancouver-school-districts-cut-staff-amid-54m-deficit/#:~:text=Facing%20multi,how%20the%20state%20funds%20schools)[\[52\]](https://katu.com/news/investigations/facing-multi-million-dollar-cuts-washington-school-districts-point-state-funding-fix-money-students-teachers-politics-budget-camas-battle-ground-clark-county-vancouver#:~:text=KATU%20learned%20the%20budget%20woes,government%20that%20patched%20the%20problem). However, Vancouver's complete exhaustion of reserves and need for emergency cash advances perhaps indicate it was on the **bleeding edge of the crisis**, making it a cautionary example watched closely by others. All districts are urging legislative action to increase funding -- a recognition that without broader fixes, these local shortfalls will continue to surface year after year[\[38\]](https://vansd.org/2024-25-budget-information/#:~:text=You%20can%20help%20us%20by,our%20Vancouver%20Public%20Schools%20students)[\[49\]](https://www.opb.org/article/2024/03/21/oregon-southwest-washington-school-districts-face-significant-budget-cuts/#:~:text=Inadequate%20funding%20from%20the%20state%2C,strikes%20%20to%20%2012).
